## Ross Simmonds — Distribution Channel Matrix

Source: *Create Once, Distribute Forever* (2024); Foundation Marketing
Distribution Checklist (90-150+ channels).

| Channel Type | Examples | Channel-User Fit Signal | Community Norm |
|---|---|---|---|
| Email — owned list | Existing subscribers | High intent; already opted in | Segment by topic relevance |
| Email — third-party / sponsorship | Newsletter ads (e.g., The Hustle, Morning Brew, vertical newsletters) | Medium; borrowed trust | Negotiate placement + CTA ownership |
| Email — 1:1 outreach | Warm personal email to industry contacts | High trust; low volume | Personalize; no mass blast |
| Email — outbound sales distribution | SDR/AE includes in active sequences | Medium; contextual use | Share as relevant resource, not attachment |
| Social — LinkedIn personal | Founder/exec/practitioner posts | High; professional context matches B2B | First-person voice; avoid corporate tone |
| Social — LinkedIn company page | Brand posts | Medium; lower organic reach | Supplement personal posts; do not replace |
| Social — Twitter/X thread | Insight threads linking to gated asset | Medium; high visibility if engaging | Hook tweet must stand alone without link |
| Community — Reddit | Relevant subreddits | High when community-fit is strong | Read rules; participate before promoting; no bare links as top-level post |
| Community — Slack/Discord | Industry-specific workspaces | High trust; niche | Earn credibility first; share in relevant channels |
| Community — Facebook Groups | Professional/interest groups | Medium (varies) | Community-first; check admin posting rules |
| Syndication — paid networks | NetLine, TechTarget, Industry Dive | High volume MoFu; pay-per-lead | Gate strong asset; enforce lead quality SLA |
| Partnership — co-marketing | Partner email to their list | High trust; borrowed audience | Reciprocal; negotiate before publication |
| Partnership — analyst/influencer | Shared with industry voices | High credibility; earned, not paid | Offer data access or attribution, not cash |
| Republication | Medium, Substack, industry blogs | Medium; SEO + new audience | Use canonical link; paraphrase or condense |

## NetLine — Format-to-Intent Table

Source: *State of B2B Content Consumption & Demand Report*, 2024 (6.2M
registrations) and 2025 (7.9M registrations).

| Content Format | Demand Level | Intent Horizon | Sales Follow-Up Guidance |
|---|---|---|---|
| eBook | Highest overall volume | Mixed — awareness to consideration | Gate; nurture sequence; SDR at score threshold |
| Trend report | High volume | 6-12 months (177% more likely to close within 12 months) | Long nurture; do not rush to SDR |
| Playbook | High volume | 3-6 months (~102% more likely within 6 months) | Mid-nurture; SDR after second touch |
| White paper | Moderate volume | ~12 months (~49% more likely within 12 months) | Nurture-first; late-stage SDR handoff |
| Case study | High conversion signal | Near-term purchase (BoFu indicator) | SDR within 48-hour window after consumption |
| Live webinar | High conversion signal | Near-term purchase (BoFu indicator) | Day-of or day-after follow-up is appropriate |
| Kit / bundle | High conversion signal | Near-term purchase association | Treat as BoFu; qualify quickly |
| Research report (original data) | High volume, differentiated | 6-12 months | Gate behind form; PR amplification as ToFu |

**Consumption Gap rule:** Average B2B professional takes ~47.7 hours between
form-fill and consuming gated content. SDR contact at time-of-fill is a cold call
with no shared context. Encode a minimum 48-hour delay in CRM/sequencer before
SDR enrollment. Send one light-touch confirmation email only before that window.

## Syndication Economics Table

Source: LeadSpot — *B2B SaaS Content Syndication Benchmarks* (2025).

| Channel | CPL Range | Lead-to-SQO Rate | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Content syndication (NetLine/TechTarget/Industry Dive) | $50-60 | ~5% | Mid-funnel researchers; need scoring + nurture |
| Paid social (LinkedIn lead gen) | $180-200+ | ~2% | Intent signal varies; CPL high |
| Paid search (branded/non-brand) | $150-250+ | 3-8% (brand: higher) | High intent on branded; expensive at scale |
| Newsletter sponsorship | Flat fee ($500-3K) | Varies by list | CPL effective for niche audiences |
| Organic/SEO | Near-zero marginal | 2-5% | Highest long-term ROI; slow to scale |

**Decision rule:** Do not select a syndication network on CPL alone. Compare
cost-per-SQO across channels. Vetted syndication networks should keep invalid
leads under 5%; if above, renegotiate the SLA or remove the network.

## Atomization Reference — Derivative Map

One pillar (research report, original data study, or definitive guide) produces:

1. Twitter/X thread — 10-15 tweets; last tweet = gated CTA link
2. LinkedIn long-form post — personal exec/founder voice; different angle than the thread
3. LinkedIn carousel — top 5-7 insights, visual slides
4. LinkedIn company page post — pull a third angle; do not replicate personal post
5. Email newsletter — your take + key takeaways; link to full asset
6. Short-form video (60-90s) — one key insight; hook in first 3 seconds
7. Podcast angle — deep dive on methodology or counter-intuitive finding
8. Guest post pitch — tailored angle for a partner or industry publication
9. SlideShare / Canva deck — visual summary
10. Infographic — one key data visualization; visual entry point to gated content
11. Quote cards (3-5) — pull best lines for social; brand-consistent design
12. Reddit post — value-first commentary; link only if community rules permit
13. Newsletter sponsorship copy (3 variations) — for paid placements in partner newsletters
14. Sales enablement card — one-pager for SDR/AE: why this asset matters to prospects
15. Webinar topic — expand one section into a 30-45 min session; use registrants as MoFu leads
