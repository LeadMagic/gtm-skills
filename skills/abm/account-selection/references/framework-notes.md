# Account Selection — Framework Notes

Use these references to ground outputs in named, repeatable methodology.

## Primary Frameworks

- **TOPO Account Selection.** TOPO's model scores accounts on three axes: ICP fit (firmographic + technographic), intent (surge signals, content consumption, third-party research activity), and relationship (existing champions, executive connections, prior engagement). The composite score determines ABM tier assignment (1:1 / 1:few / 1:many) and sales routing priority.

- **Winning by Design — ICP Framework.** WbD's ICP methodology ties account selection to the Bowtie model: an account is only "selected" if it passes both acquisition-fit AND retention-fit gates. An account that buys but churns in 6 months was selected wrong — the Bowtie's expansion stage must be part of selection criteria.

- **ITSMA — Account-Based Marketing.** ITSMA's account selection principle: the TAM that fits your ICP is your ABM universe; the account list you can actually engage with your available capacity is your ABM target. Selection must constrain scope to what can be executed — a list of 500 accounts with 1 AE is a wish list.

- **Lars Nilsson — ABSD Signal-Based Selection.** Nilsson's account-based sales development model prioritizes accounts showing active signals: funding events, leadership changes, tech stack migrations, job postings. Static firmographic lists without signal layers miss accounts that are in-market now while wasting effort on perfect-fit accounts that aren't buying.

## Operating Assumptions

- Account selection = fit score + intent signal + relationship access. All three axes must be scored.
- Tier assignment (1:1 / 1:few / 1:many / nurture) should be output of the scoring model, not an input assumption.
- Selection is a quarterly process, not annual: accounts graduate or drop between tiers based on signal changes.
