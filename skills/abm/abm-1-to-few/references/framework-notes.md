# Abm 1 To Few — Framework Notes

Use these references to ground outputs in named, repeatable methodology.

## Primary Frameworks

- **TOPO ABM Scale Framework.** TOPO's 1:few ABM model (15-50 accounts) clusters accounts by shared characteristics — industry vertical, use case, buying trigger, or competitor displacement — to achieve ~80% of 1:1 personalization at ~20% of the effort. The cluster is the scaling unit: each cluster gets semi-custom content, a shared playbook, and persona variants.

- **Force Management — MEDDICC.** MEDDICC's Champion and Committee elements are critical at 1:few scale because you're orchestrating engagement across buying committee members within multiple clusters simultaneously. The 4-part champion test (power, win, articulation, internal sell) must succeed across industries, not just within one account.

- **ITSMA — Account-Based Marketing.** ITSMA's tier definition for 1:few: moderate account investment ($5K-$15K per account annually), semi-custom content per cluster, cluster-specific events (industry roundtables, vertical webinars), and BDR/AE coordination within each cluster.

## Operating Assumptions

- 15-50 accounts clustered by shared characteristics; each cluster gets industry/use-case-specific plays.
- Personalization lives at the cluster level (industry pain, competitor landscape, use case proof) with individual account variables (contact name, company stat, specific tech stack).
- The cluster is the pacing unit: campaign cadence, content refresh, and measurement should be per-cluster, not per-account.
