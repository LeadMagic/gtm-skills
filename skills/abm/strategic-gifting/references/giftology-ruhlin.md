# Giftology — John Ruhlin

**John Ruhlin** authored *Giftology* and built Ruhlin Group — strategic gifting for
relationship-driven sales (Cleveland Cavaliers model, enterprise advisors).

**Public:** [Giftology book](https://www.giftologybook.com/) · Ruhlin Group

## Core Principles

### 1. Go deep, not wide

One remarkable gift to a decision-maker > 100 branded items. Concentrate budget on
people who matter to the deal.

### 2. The 3-Gift System

| Gift | Recipient | Purpose |
|---|---|---|
| **Gift 1** | The buyer | Personal — reflects *their* interests |
| **Gift 2** | Their family | Spouse/kids — "advocate at home" |
| **Gift 3** | Their team | Shared experience — respect for org |

### 3. Personalization over price

$100 gift that shows research (alma mater, hobby, quote from call) > $500 generic basket.

**Research sources:** LinkedIn, podcast appearances, company bio, champion intel, social.

### 4. Story is the gift

Connect to conversation: "When you mentioned [X] on our call, I thought of this."

The object is delivery; the **story** is what they remember.

### 5. No logo on relationship gifts

Logo = advertising. Giftology gifts are about **them**, not your brand.

### 6. Handwritten note

3 sentences by hand. Typed notes feel mass-produced.

## What Giftology is NOT

- Trade show swag
- Holiday card blasts
- Bribery to force signature
- Review incentives (G2 ToS — see `review-platforms`)

## Ruhlin vs Sendoso

| Ruhlin (Giftology) | Sendoso (platform) |
|---|---|
| Strategy, personalization, story | Logistics, eGift, warehouse, CRM |
| Tier 1 concierge gifts | Tier 2–3 scaled sending |
| Manual research intensive | Address confirm, analytics |

**Always:** Giftology brief first → then Sendoso (or manual) to execute.
