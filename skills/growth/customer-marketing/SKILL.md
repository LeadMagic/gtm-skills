# Customer Marketing

## Overview
Customer marketing turns users into advocates. Advocates provide references, case studies, testimonials, and word-of-mouth that outperform any paid channel. This skill builds programs that identify, cultivate, and activate customer advocates.

## When to Use
- "Build a customer advocacy program"
- "Collect customer testimonials"
- "Create a reference program"
- "Drive customer-driven growth"

## Step-by-Step Process
### Phase 1: Advocate Identification
Score customers on: NPS, product usage depth, tenure, executive relationship strength, willingness to participate (survey). Top 10-20% are potential advocates.

### Phase 2: Advocacy Ladder
Level 1: Review/G2 rating (low effort). Level 2: Quote/Testimonial. Level 3: Case Study. Level 4: Reference Call. Level 5: Speaking/Webinar/Content co-creation. Move advocates up the ladder over time.

### Phase 3: Activation
Personal outreach to top advocates. Make it easy (pre-written reviews, interview-based case studies). Incentivize appropriately (gift card, conference pass, co-marketing).

## Output Format
Customer marketing program design with advocate scoring model, activation playbook, and measurement framework.

## Common Pitfalls
1. **Asking too much too soon** — start with a review, not a reference call.
2. **No advocate pipeline** — identify and cultivate before you need them.
3. **One-and-done** — advocacy is an ongoing relationship, not a transaction.
