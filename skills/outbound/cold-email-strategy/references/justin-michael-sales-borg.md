## Sales Borg philosophy — what it means

Michael's first book frames modern outbound as a **cyborg operating model**, not "replace reps with bots" or "ignore automation."

| Side | Machines think and do | Humans feel and engage |
|---|---|---|
| **Best at** | Data volume, signal detection, trigger monitoring, repetitive tasks, A/B tests, rudimentary personalization at scale, recommendations | Genuine empathy, belief transfer, trust, relevant storytelling, strategic deal judgment |
| **Failure mode** | Spammy volume, vanity personalization, burning TAM, commodity transactions | Manual drudgery, no TQ, "another frickin' login," 36% selling time |

**When to use Sales Borg:** You have proven ICP, personas, and messaging — then automate top-of-funnel workflow while reserving humans for high-stakes engagement, referrals, and complex discovery. **When not to:** Before ICP/messaging is nailed; strategic enterprise deals where hyper-personalization beats scale; brand-sensitive segments where over-automation damages trust.

> "We believe humans need to become more like machines and machines need to become more like humans." — Justin Michael ([Salesborgs.ai](https://www.salesborgs.ai/))

**RevOps implication:** Ops becomes **orchestrator of bots** — CRM, MAP, SEP, enrichment, and dialers synced as one revenue system (*Smarketing*: sales + marketing journey mapped across inbound/outbound touchpoints).

## Technology Quotient (TQ)

TQ = ability to assimilate and apply sales technology (HBR definition cited in TPS). Without TQ, expensive stack = unused gym membership.

| TQ level | Behavior | Outcome |
|---|---|---|
| **Laggard** | Logs into SEP occasionally; manual list building | 1% cold email reply rates; AFL stack |
| **Superuser** | Certified on SEP + CRM; snippets, rulesets, A/B tests, integrations | One sequencing master can eclipse 6–15 reps (TPS claim) |
| **Orchestrator (RevOps)** | Tray/Zapier/API glue; suppression + governance; signal → action routing | Pipeline at fraction of headcount |

**Stack tiers (TPS Part I):** CRM/CX · social intelligence (LinkedIn Nav) · SEP (Outreach/SalesLoft/etc.) · enrichment · trigger monitoring · parallel dialers · collaboration. Advanced: intent, technographics, conversation intelligence, scheduling (Chili Piper), reply sentiment.

**Benchmark spend reference:** ~$1,000/rep/month on high-growth startup stacks (TPS) — validate against your ACV and motion.

## Human vs bot division of labor

| Workflow step | Bot / automation | Human |
|---|---|---|
| List build & hygiene | Enrichment waterfalls, trigger feeds, ICP filters | Define ICP, persona docs, suppression rules |
| Signal detection | Intent, job change, funding, technographic alerts | Validate signal relevance; exclude false positives |
| Sequence enrollment | SEP cadences, branch logic, send-time tests | Approve messaging; pause on strategic accounts |
| Email/LinkedIn copy (Tier 1–2) | Snippets, merge fields, ML injectables (Nova-style) | Tier 3 executive touches; referral intros |
| Reply classification | Sentiment tags, auto-route rules | All positive, pricing, legal, procurement replies |
| Meeting booking | Calendly/Chili Piper, reminder bots | Live discovery; executive alignment |
| AE handoff | CRM fields, "high opens," referral notes | Triple-touch follow-up (call + VM + email <90 sec) |

**Part III caveat (TPS):** A salesbot *can* replace humans for **commodity** buying — not for complex B2B value selling. Semi-automation is the realistic ceiling for enterprise today.

## Targeting — attributes vs triggers

| Concept | Definition | Use |
|---|---|---|
| **Attribute** | Firmographic/psychographic fit — defines ICP | Size TAM, segment ABM buckets |
| **Trigger event** | Change that creates *why now* | Opens the conversation with context |

**Four trigger types (TPS):** bad experience with incumbent · role-based change · change in results/strategy · operating environment change. **Strongest B2B trigger:** decision-maker role change (new exec mandate to improve).

**Power combo:** trigger + referral = "sales superpower." Cold generic ROI claims underperform trigger-anchored relevance.

**Buy reasons (narrow to four):** make money · save money · reduce risk · maintain compliance.

## Messaging & sequencing at scale

### Value narrative (before any automation)

1. Lead with **target persona's opportunity** — never open with you, your company, or your product.
2. **Relevant personalization** > vanity demographics — pain-linked, not "I saw you went to State U."
3. **Brevity:** 1–3 word subject lines (action words like "growth"); 3–5 sentences max; CTA often as sentence three.
4. **REPLY methodology:** Relevant results · Empathy · Personalization · Laser-focus — make *them* the hero, not buyer-oriented "we" copy.

### Sequence vs cadence

- **Sequence** = order of touches (message + channel).
- **Cadence** = rhythm/frequency between touches.
- Build **high-touch flows** when prospects need more education before converting.

### Design, test, refine (TPS Part II)

Split-test: time of day · day of week · subject length · ultra-short vs long copy · all lowercase. Future state: sequencer auto-routes leads to winning bucket.

**Visual outbound:** ~80% of email is visual — inline GIFs, diagrams, short infographics spike opens (CloudApp-style).

### Phone complement — ROUTE / RUIN / MULTIPLY

| Move | Purpose |
|---|---|
| **ROUTE** | Find problem owner; get referred |
| **RUIN** | Pull thread on incumbent satisfaction |
| **MULTIPLY** | Sell augmentation vs rip-and-replace |

Open with "How have you been?" outperforming "Did I catch you at a bad time?" (Gong data cited in TPS).

## SDR / AE workflow with automation layers

```
[Data lake: ICP + triggers + enrichment]
        ↓
[SEP: multi-channel cadence + A/B branches]
        ↓
[SDR: reply handling, referral capture, "signs of life"]
        ↓
[AE: triple high-intent within 90 sec]
        ↓
[CRM: single source of truth + suppression]
```

| Role | KPI focus (TPS benchmarks) | Automation leverage |
|---|---|---|
| **SDR/BDR** | OAC 1.5–10%; OCC 0.5–2.5%; reply 5–10% min; opens 30–50%+ | ~200 accounts/month effective (Bridge Group cite); PAD dialers 5→20–40 live convos/day |
| **AE** | Conversion 3–6% of contacted; speed on warm handoffs | Shared Nav lists; HVS/Einstein next-best-action |
| **RevOps** | Input metrics that matter; 30-day tool ramp | Data governance, GDPR/CCPA, DNC, suppression sync CRM↔SEP |

**ABP:** Always be prospecting — async sequences keep pipeline warm while humans focus on aligned conversations.

## Anti-patterns

| Anti-pattern | Why it fails | Fix |
|---|---|---|
| Automate before ICP/messaging | Sequences amplify bad message at scale | Document personas + "why change/why now/why you" first |
| Attention-getting fluff personalization | No pain link → wasted touches | REPLY + trigger context |
| Burn entire TAM quickly | Universe exhaustion | Segment ABM-style; cap enrollment velocity |
| Copy-paste multi-channel | Same text on email + LinkedIn = annoyance | Channel-native touches (see `multi-channel-outreach`) |
| False hope / "maybe" | Kills forecast | Assertive cadence; welcome clear No/Refer |
| Over-automation on strategic deals | Damages brand on high-value targets | Human layer for Tier 1 accounts |
| Ignoring replies | All responses are "signs of life" | Answer every reply fast; route referrals immediately |
| Connect-and-sell on LinkedIn | Platform closes automation doors | Connect → value → then outreach |
| Measuring opens alone | Vanity without conversion | Reply rate, OAC/OCC, meetings, pipeline |

## Setup checklist (Sales Borg outbound)

**Foundation (Week 0)**
- [ ] ICP doc with 3–5 personas (demographics, goals, objections, proof, why change/now/you)
- [ ] Attribute vs trigger catalog wired to data sources
- [ ] Suppression: customers, competitors, DNC, opt-outs synced CRM ↔ SEP
- [ ] Value narrative peer-reviewed — no product-first opens

**Stack (Week 1)**
- [ ] CRM + SEP + enrichment + trigger feed integrated (API/Zapier/Tray glue)
- [ ] SEP certifications for operators (Outreach/SalesLoft university or equivalent)
- [ ] Chili Piper or scheduling equivalent on positive replies
- [ ] Conversation intelligence on live calls (optional but TPS-recommended at scale)

**Cadence (Week 2)**
- [ ] 4–7 touch sequence with channel mix documented
- [ ] A/B test plan for subject + body variants
- [ ] Reply taxonomy + AE handoff SLA (<90 sec on hot referrals)
- [ ] Daily send caps aligned with deliverability (`email-deliverability`)

**Governance (ongoing)**
- [ ] RevOps owns bot orchestration + data hygiene
- [ ] Weekly review: reply rate, OAC/OCC, bad-fit replies, TAM burn rate
- [ ] Re-enroll rules after breakup (90-day pause default)

## Daily workflow (individual rep)

| Block | Activity | Borg layer |
|---|---|---|
| **Morning** | Review trigger queue + overnight replies | Machine-surfaced priority list |
| **Block 1** | Execute SEP tasks (emails, LinkedIn steps) | Automated sends; human approves Tier 1 |
| **Block 2** | Live calls / PAD sessions | Human; ROUTE/RUIN/MULTIPLY |
| **Block 3** | Reply handling + referrals + AE handoffs | Human judgment; auto-classification assists |
| **Block 4** | A/B review + snippet refinement | Human strategy; machine metrics |
| **EOD** | Log outcomes; update suppression | CRM sync |

Target: **80% time selling** vs industry ~36% (TPS) — automation buys back research/admin.
