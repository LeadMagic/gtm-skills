## Core Thesis — "Earn the Right"

Most modern outbound is **"Legacy Outbound"**: high-volume, low-value, entitlement-driven — it assumes the prospect owes you their time, and AI is being used to do more bad outreach at scale. Venetz's counter is **buyer-first outbound**: cold outbound isn't dead, it's done badly. The fix is a single discipline applied to **every** touch:

> "Have I earned the right to make this ask of my prospect? If that ask is to read an email, what have I said in this email that delivered something relevant and valuable enough to earn their time and attention?" — Leslie Venetz ([The B2B Playbook](https://theb2bplaybook.com/outbound-sales-strategy); [Common Room](https://www.commonroom.io/blog/beyond-intent-leslie-venetz/))

If you can't answer that compellingly, **go in for another round of audits before sending** — don't send. Trust is the foundation of the sale, and the primary currency for earning trust/attention is the **quality of your listening** ([Authority Magazine](https://medium.com/authority-magazine/leslie-venetz-of-the-sales-led-gtm-agency-on-the-hidden-superpower-of-every-great-leader-70cacab2c77f)).

**Complementary lane:** Use Venetz for the **buyer-first targeting + messaging discipline and the trust/relevance audit** that gates a campaign. Pair with Eric Nowoslawski (infra/economics/offer scale), Pat Spielmann (Hook-Line-Sinker copy skeleton + enrichment), Jordan Crawford (PQS segment research), Becc Holland (diagnostic email structure), and Guillaume Moubeche (lemlist multichannel mechanics). Venetz overlaps most with the "spam-the-TAM vs relevance" debate — she owns the **earn-the-right gate and problem-centric audit**, not the deliverability infra.

## Primary Frameworks (Public)

### 1. Earn the Right — the pre-send audit

Apply to every channel and touch. Before sending, the message must contain something **relevant and valuable to the buyer** that justifies the ask. Outreach that is mostly about what *you* want (a call, a demo, a reply) has not earned it.

| Gate question | Pass condition |
|---|---|
| Have I earned the right to ask for their attention? | The touch delivers a buyer-relevant insight/value before any ask |
| Is the ask proportional to what I've given? | Touch 1 asks for a thought/reply, not 30 minutes |
| Would this read as entitled? | No "you owe me your time because I have your email" energy |

**Source:** [The B2B Playbook](https://theb2bplaybook.com/outbound-sales-strategy) · [Common Room](https://www.commonroom.io/blog/beyond-intent-leslie-venetz/) · [Jeff Bajorek newsletter](https://newsletter.jeffbajorek.com/p/my-chat-with-leslie-venetz)

### 2. Profit-Generating Pipeline — 9-step formula

The book's promise: a **9-step proven formula to earn trust, build a better pipeline, and drive revenue** — replacing "salesy," legacy tactics with a repeatable, buyer-centric outbound process. Publicly documented pillars an agent can apply (full step list is in the book):

- **Earn the Right mindset** as the operating philosophy (team + buyer trust).
- **Modernize** away from spray-and-pray; build **repeatable** processes for sustainable pipeline.
- **Problem-centric (not product-centric) messaging** (see #3).
- **Deep segmentation** so relevance is possible at all (see #4).
- **Strengthen top-of-funnel** for higher-quality leads, measured on trust/buyer signals.

Do **not** invent specific step names that aren't public; cite the book for the full sequence.

**Source:** [Porchlight listing](https://www.porchlightbooks.com/products/profit-generating-pipeline-leslie-venetz-9798998527036) · [salesledgtm.com/book](https://salesledgtm.com/book)

### 3. Product-centric → Problem-centric copy audit (the I/we/our test)

A deterministic copy audit any agent can run:

1. Go through the copy and count **"I / we / our"** vs **"you / yours / they / them."**
2. Target roughly a **1:3 ratio** — talk about the buyer ~3x for every 1x you talk about yourself.
3. Eliminate self-centered openers: *"We sell… / We do… / Our company… / I noticed…"* Replace with the buyer's reality and problem.

> "Take some of the pressure off yourself to make every email perfect… that's why you have multiple touchpoints across multiple channels — increasing the chance you get the right message to the right person on the right channel at the right time." — Leslie Venetz ([Jeff Bajorek newsletter](https://newsletter.jeffbajorek.com/p/my-chat-with-leslie-venetz))

A great cold email is **clear, concise, and centered on the buyer** — not long or clever.

**Source:** [Jeff Bajorek newsletter](https://newsletter.jeffbajorek.com/p/my-chat-with-leslie-venetz) · [Sales-Led GTM podcast Ep. 187](https://www.buzzsprout.com/2245025/episodes/18056360-episode-187-building-a-profit-generating-pipeline-with-leslie-venetz)

### 4. Deep segmentation / signal stacking — "I only want the moose"

Relevance is impossible without segmentation. Stack **enough firmographics + intent signals + persona/context-specific facts** that you can be confident the message is highly relevant to that slice. Don't try to message "all forest animals" — decide you want the moose, then learn what matters to the moose.

- Combine firmographics with **intent signals / triggers** (timeliness) — not static-list-only.
- Still a numbers game: even with great targeting you must reach **enough of the right people at the right time**.

**Source:** [Common Room](https://www.commonroom.io/blog/beyond-intent-leslie-venetz/) · [Jeff Bajorek newsletter](https://newsletter.jeffbajorek.com/p/my-chat-with-leslie-venetz)

### 5. TAM → ICP narrowing (founder / early-stage lens)

Founders default to TAM-speak (their "1M potential prospects") because that's how they pitch VCs. For outbound, narrow to **ICP** (demographics, firmographics, behaviors, psychographics), then segment to the small subset most likely to **buy now, at the highest price point, and renew.**

> "All you really need to get right is outreach to ~380 people, because you only really need ~10 of them to say yes." — Leslie Venetz ([Founders Network](https://foundersnetwork.com/how-to-excel-at-founder-led-sales-with-leslie-venetz/))

Applies equally to founder cold outreach and founder emails to VCs: write the message they'll actually reply to.

**Source:** [Founders Network — Founder-led Sales](https://foundersnetwork.com/how-to-excel-at-founder-led-sales-with-leslie-venetz/)

### 6. Three Channel Minimum + "double-tap"

Choose **at least three** outbound channels (email / LinkedIn / phone are the common stack). Multiple touchpoints across channels raise the odds of "right message, right person, right channel, right time." Use a **double-tap** — a close follow-up touch — to lift recall without being aggressive. Take the pressure off any single message being perfect.

**Source:** [Common Room](https://www.commonroom.io/blog/beyond-intent-leslie-venetz/) · Thinkers360 — "Three Channel Minimum" article (Apr 2025)

### 7. Trust as the ultimate KPI

Most "messaging / pipeline / conversion" problems are really a **trust gap**, and under that, a **listening gap**. Track **buyer-led signals**, not just booked meetings; the cure is relevance and listening, not more volume.

**Source:** [The B2B Playbook](https://theb2bplaybook.com/outbound-sales-strategy) · [Authority Magazine](https://medium.com/authority-magazine/leslie-venetz-of-the-sales-led-gtm-agency-on-the-hidden-superpower-of-every-great-leader-70cacab2c77f)

## Anti-Patterns

| Mistake | Why it fails (Venetz) | Fix |
|---|---|---|
| Entitled "you owe me your time" outreach | Legacy Outbound; erodes brand + trust | Earn-the-Right gate before send |
| Product-centric copy ("we / our / I") | Reads as self-serving; deleted | I/we/our → you/them audit to ~1:3 |
| AI to scale bad outreach | Amplifies irrelevance | Use AI to respect the relationship, not to do more bad stuff faster |
| Spray-the-TAM lists | No relevance possible | Segment + stack signals ("only the moose") |
| Founder talks in TAM | Vague, low-reply | Narrow to ICP → ~380 most-likely buyers |
| Optimizing for booked meetings only | Misses the trust gap | Track buyer-led signals; lead with listening |
| Trying to make one email perfect | False pressure; single point of failure | Multiple touches across ≥3 channels |
