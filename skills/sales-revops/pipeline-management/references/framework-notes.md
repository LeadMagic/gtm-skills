# Pipeline Management — Framework Notes

## SPICED + MEDDICC Reconciliation

These frameworks do not contradict — they operate at different layers:

| SPICED (WbD) | Maps To MEDDICC | Shared CRM Field |
|---|---|---|
| Situation | (context for all dimensions) | `current_state` |
| Pain | Identify Pain (I) | `identified_pain` |
| Impact | Metrics (M) | `success_metrics`, `impact_annual_cost` |
| Critical Event | Pain urgency + Decision Process timing | `why_now`, `deadline_date` |
| Decision | Economic Buyer + Decision Criteria + Decision Process | `economic_buyer_name`, `decision_criteria`, `decision_process_steps` |

**Rule:** SPICED in discovery conversations (Connect → Discovery).
MEDDICC scorecard from Solution stage onward. Same CRM fields — one source of truth.

## Andy Whyte — MEDDICC Scorecard

Score 0 / 1 / 2 per dimension (max 14). Re-score after every customer touch.

| Dim | 0 Unknown | 1 Suspected | 2 Confirmed |
|---|---|---|---|
| M Metrics | No KPIs discussed | KPIs named, not quantified | Baseline + target documented |
| E Economic Buyer | Not identified | Named, not met | Met directly, budget confirmed |
| D Decision Criteria | Unknown | Partial list | Documented, your strengths mapped |
| D Decision Process | Unknown | Steps named | Dates, approvers, sequence mapped |
| I Identify Pain | No pain | Pain stated | Quantified cost of inaction |
| C Champion | No champion | Enthusiast only | Passes four-part Champion test |
| C Competition | Unknown | Competitors named | Status quo + build/buy assessed |

### Champion Test (Whyte)
- Power to influence (not just a fan)
- Personal win defined
- Articulates problem without vendor help
- Commits to internal selling

### Stage Gates
- Solution: min 6/14, Pain=2
- Proposal: min 10/14, Champion=2, EB≥1
- Negotiation: min 12/14, EB=2
- Commit forecast: min 13/14

## Winning by Design — GTM Playbook Kit

Goal + In-Stage Actions + Exit Criteria per stage.
