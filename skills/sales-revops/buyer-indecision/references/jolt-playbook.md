# JOLT Playbook — Buyer Indecision

**Source:** Matthew Dixon & Ted McKenna, *The JOLT Effect* (Portfolio, 2022)  
https://www.jolteffect.com/

## Research Anchors

- 40–60% of B2B deals lost to **no decision**
- 56% of no-decision losses = **indecision** (FOMU), not status quo preference
- High performers shift from "sell upside" to **reduce downside** after intent forms

## FOMU vs Objection

| Buyer Says | Likely Issue | Response Family |
|---|---|---|
| "Too expensive" | Value objection | `objection-handling` |
| "Need to think about it" | Indecision / FOMU | **JOLT** |
| "Happy with incumbent" | Status quo | Challenger teach + SPICED pain |
| "Need more features" | Exploration overload | **Limit** (JOLT L) |

## J — Judge Indecision

**Diagnostic questions:**
1. "What happens if you do nothing for the next quarter?"
2. "Who besides you feels the risk if this goes wrong?"
3. "What would need to be true for this to feel like a safe yes?"

**Indecision types:**
- **Personal FOMU** — champion's career risk
- **Committee FOMU** — no one wants to sign
- **Analysis paralysis** — endless evaluation
- **Passive resistors** — silent blockers on buying team

## O — Offer Recommendation

**Talk track:**
> "If I were in your role, accountable for [metric], I would start with [specific package]. That's the path I'd recommend."

**Champion enablement:** Give internal email draft + 1-page "why this, why now."

## L — Limit Exploration

- Max **2 options** in proposal
- Time-box decision: "If we align by [date], we can hold implementation slot [date]."
- Remove optional modules from default proposal

## T — Take Risk Off the Table

| Mechanism | Script Element |
|---|---|
| Phased pilot | "Start with [team]; expand on [criteria]" |
| Mutual success plan | Joint doc with milestones + review dates |
| Executive alignment | EB + your exec 30-min "partnership" call |
| Out clause / pilot terms | Defined in `deal-desk` packages |
| Reference call | Peer in same industry who de-risked |

## Champion Internal Sell Kit

1. One-page recommendation (O)
2. Risk removal summary (T)
3. Decision timeline (L)
4. SPICED Decision criteria recap
