# Pitch Deck Builder

## Overview
A pitch deck tells the story of why change is necessary and why your solution is the answer. The best decks make one argument, support it with proof, and end with a clear next step.

## When to Use
- "Build a pitch deck" / "Create a sales presentation" / "Make an investor deck"

## Step-by-Step Process
### Phase 1: Deck Architecture
1. Title | 2. The Problem | 3. Cost of the Problem | 4. The Shift (market change) | 5. Our Approach | 6. Product Walkthrough (3-4 workflows) | 7. Proof Points (metrics, logos) | 8. Case Study | 9. ROI / Value | 10. Pricing Overview | 11. Next Steps / CTA

### Phase 2: Persona Customization
CTO: architecture, security, integration depth. VP Sales: revenue impact, ramp time, rep productivity. CEO: strategic alignment, competitive moat, vision.

### Phase 3: Speaker Notes
Every slide: what to say, when to pause, what questions to ask.

## Output Format
Complete deck outline with slide headlines, body copy, speaker notes, and visual direction per slide.

## Common Pitfalls
1. **Feature dump** — slides listing features without outcomes. Every slide answers "so what?"
2. **One deck for everyone** — different personas need different emphasis. CTO deck ≠ VP Sales deck.
3. **No speaker notes** — slides without delivery guidance produce inconsistent presentations.
4. **Too many slides** — 10-11 is the sweet spot. 25 slides = nobody remembers anything.
