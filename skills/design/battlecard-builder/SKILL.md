# Battlecard Builder

## Overview
A battlecard gives reps exactly what to say in competitive situations. Not a feature comparison table — a talk track with proof points. The FIA Framework (Fact → Impact → Act) structures every card for immediate use in conversation.

## When to Use
- "Build a battlecard against [competitor]" / "Create competitive battlecards"

## Step-by-Step Process
### Phase 1: Card Types
- Why We Win: Key advantages with talk tracks
- Why We Lose: Where they win, how to counter
- Objection Handling: Competitor-specific objections with responses
- Pricing Card: Pricing comparison with value positioning
- Quick Dismiss: One-card summary

### Phase 2: FIA Structure
- Fact: The competitive insight (what is true)
- Impact: Why it matters to the deal (so what)
- Act: What the rep says or does (the talk track)

### Phase 3: Per-Competitor Package
For each major competitor: Why We Win, Why We Lose, Objection Handling, Pricing, and Quick Dismiss cards.

## Output Format
Complete battlecard set per competitor with FIA-structured cards, talk tracks, and proof points.

## Common Pitfalls
1. **Feature comparison instead of talk tracks** — rep cannot say a feature table in a call.
2. **No proof points** — claims without evidence lose to competitors who have them.
3. **One card for all competitors** — different competitors require different positioning.
4. **Stale data** — battlecards with old pricing are worse than no battlecard.
