Skipping it\nis the single most expensive mistake in outbound — a 5% bounce rate triggers\nautomated spam filtering at every major inbox provider. Recovery takes months.\n\nThe rule: every email address entering a sequence must pass verification first.\nNo exceptions. Verify when you find, re-verify every 90 days, and always verify\nbefore a new campaign launches.\n\n## When to Use\n\n- \"Verify these emails before we send\"\n- \"Check if these addresses are deliverable\"\n- \"Clean my prospect list\"\n- \"Are these safe to send to?\"\n- \"Validate this CSV of contacts\"\n- \"What's the bounce risk on this list?\"\n\nDo NOT use for:\n- Finding new emails — use email-finding first\n- Deliverability infrastructure setup — use email-deliverability\n- General list hygiene beyond email — use list-building\n\n## Authoritative Foundations\n\nEmail verification is grounded in established deliverability practices.\nIndustry consensus across major sending platforms (Google Workspace, M365,\nSmartlead, Instantly) converges on a bounce rate ceiling of 2% for cold\noutreach and best-in-class operations targeting under 1%.\n\nData decay is real: 2-3% of B2B emails become invalid every month. A\n10,000-contact database loses 200-300 valid emails monthly. Re-verification\non a 90-day cadence is the minimum to maintain list health.\n\n## Prerequisites\n\n- A list of email addresses to verify\n- Access to a verification service (LeadMagic Email Validation, ZeroBounce,\n  NeverBounce, MillionVerifier, or equivalent)\n- For batch verification: CSV with at minimum an email column\n\n## Step-by-Step Process\n\n### Phase 1: Intake\n\nAsk the user:\n1. How many emails need verification? (single vs batch)\n2. When were these emails last verified? (if never, flag as high-risk)\n3. What's the sending volume and cadence? (higher volume = stricter verification)\n4. Are these going to cold outreach or warm nurture? (cold = stricter)\n\n### Phase 2: Run Verification\n\nSubmit each email to the verification service. Services return a status:\n\n| Status | Meaning | Action for Cold Outbound | Action for Warm/Nurture |\n|---|---|---|---|\n| **valid** | Mailbox exists and accepts mail | Safe to send | Safe to send |\n| **invalid** | Mailbox doesn't exist or is deactivated | Do not send — remove from list | Do not send |\n| **risky / catch-all** | Domain accepts all mail, can't confirm individual mailbox | Skip at volume. Send selectively (max 5% of volume) with extra monitoring | Send, monitor bounces closely |\n| **unknown** | Could not determine status | Skip. Re-verify in 7 days or discard | Send if high-value, monitor |\n| **disposable** | Temporary/throwaway address | Never send | Never send |\n\n### Phase 3: Segment and Route\n\n1. **Valid** → Route to the sendable list. Mark with verification date.\n2. **Invalid** → Remove from all sequences. Log the bounce reason if available.\n3. **Risky/Catch-All** → Separate list. For cold outbound at volume: skip entirely.\n   For high-value enterprise accounts: send at reduced volume (<5% of total),\n   monitor bounce rates daily.\n4. **Unknown** → Hold for re-verification. If critical contact, attempt after 7 days.\n   Otherwise, discard.\n\n### Phase 4: Record Keeping\n\nEvery verified contact should have:\n- `verification_status`: valid | invalid | risky | unknown\n- `verification_date`: ISO date of last verification\n- `verification_source`: which service was used\n\nCRM systems should filter sequences to only include `verification_status = valid`\nAND `verification_date` within last 90 days.\n\n## Output Format\n\nSummary report:\n```\nTotal verified: 1,000\nValid: 920 (92%)\nInvalid: 35 (3.5%)\nRisky/Catch-All: 30 (3%)\nUnknown: 15 (1.5%)\n\nSendable (valid): 920\nRequires monitoring (risky): 30\nRemoved (invalid + unknown): 50\n\nEstimated safe send volume: 920-950/day (depending on risky handling)\nBounce rate projection: 0.8-1.5%\n```\n\n## Quality Check\n\n- [ ] All emails in sequence have verification_status = valid\n- [ ] No emails with verification_date older than 90 days\n- [ ] Invalid emails removed from all active sequences\n- [ ] Catch-all emails tracked separately with daily bounce monitoring\n- [ ] Disposable emails permanently suppressed\n\n## Common Pitfalls\n\n1. **Skipping verification entirely.** \"We found the emails from a reputable\n   source.\" Every provider returns stale data. 5-15% of \"found\" emails bounce.\n   The cost of re-warming a domain far exceeds the cost of verification.\n\n2. **Treating catch-all as safe.** Catch-all domains accept mail to any address\n   at the domain, making individual mailbox verification impossible. They produce\n   higher bounce rates. Treat them as a separate, monitored segment.\n\n3. **One-time verification.** \"We verified this list 6 months ago.\" At 2-3%\n   monthly decay, 12-18% of that list is now invalid. Re-verify every 90 days.\n\n4. **Sending to invalid emails one more time.** \"Maybe it was a temporary\n   bounce.\" If verification returns invalid, the mailbox doesn't exist. Sending\n   again won't fix it. Remove permanently.\n\n5. **Not updating CRM with verification status.** Verification without recording\n   the result means the data decays silently. Update CRM fields immediately.\n\n## Related Skills\n\n- **email-finding**: Find emails before verifying them\n- **email-deliverability**: Full deliverability strategy beyond verification\n- **list-building**: Build and maintain clean prospect lists\n- **deliverability-monitoring**: Ongoing monitoring of bounce rates and reputation\n
