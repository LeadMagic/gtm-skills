# Content-Led Growth

## Overview

Content is the only GTM channel that compounds. Paid ads stop when budget stops.
Outbound stops when SDRs stop. Content from 2022 is still generating pipeline
in 2026. The mistake: treating content as "blog posts" instead of a full-stack
growth engine that spans social, newsletter, long-form, podcast, community,
and paid amplification. This skill covers building a content engine that
generates pipeline, not just page views.

## Authoritative Foundations

### Steph Smith — Content as Compound Interest
- Most content ROI is invisible for 12-18 months
- One great piece = 10 derivative pieces across channels
- Distribution is 80% of the work. Writing is 20%.
- "The best content marketers are the best repurposers."

### Harry Dry — Show, Don't Tell
- Every claim needs visual proof: screenshots, data, specific examples
- "Marketing Examples" grew to 100K+ subscribers with zero ads
- Principle: "Prove it, don't claim it"

### Amanda Natividad — Zero-Click Content
- Give the value in-platform (social post, newsletter, podcast)
- Don't gate everything behind links
- Platform algorithms reward content that keeps users on-platform
- Trade-off: less traffic, more trust

## Content Architecture

### The Content Pyramid

```
Level 1: CORE ASSET (1x/month)
  Long-form article, research report, definitive guide, framework
  — repurposed into everything below

Level 2: DERIVATIVE (2-3x/week)
  Social threads, newsletter editions, podcast episodes, video clips
  — extracted from Level 1 assets

Level 3: ATOMS (daily)
  Quotes, screenshots, data points, hot takes, replies
  — pulled from Level 2, posted natively
```

### Channel Mix by Audience Type

| Audience | Primary Channel | Secondary | Content Type |
|---|---|---|---|
| Technical founders | X/Twitter, Hacker News | Newsletter, GitHub | Build in public, architecture deep dives |
| Revenue leaders | LinkedIn | Newsletter, podcast | Frameworks, playbooks, data analysis |
| Marketers | Newsletter, LinkedIn | Podcast, community | Case studies, templates, swipe files |
| Sales leaders | LinkedIn, podcast | Newsletter | Scripts, frameworks, competitive intel |
| Investors | X/Twitter, LinkedIn | Podcast, newsletter | Data-driven insights, contrarian takes |

## Step-by-Step Process

### Phase 1: Define Your Content Strategy

**Answer these 5 questions before creating anything:**

1. **Who is the audience?** Not "everyone." One ICP. Example: "Bootstrapped
   SaaS founders at $500K-5M ARR who are technical and hate marketing."

2. **What is the promise?** What will they get from following you? Example:
   "How to build GTM infrastructure that scales without a marketing team."

3. **What is the unique angle?** Your contrarian take or unique data. Example:
   "Most GTM advice is for funded startups. Here's how bootstrappers do it
   with 10% of the budget and 2x the efficiency."

4. **What is the content format?** Pick 1-2 primary formats. Don't start with
   podcast + newsletter + YouTube + TikTok. Start with ONE. Add channels when
   the first one is working.

5. **What is the pipeline mechanism?** How does content → pipeline?
   - Newsletter → gated asset → demo request
   - Social → DM → discovery call
   - Podcast → guest's audience → inbound
   - Community → trust → referral

### Phase 2: The Core Asset Engine

**Monthly core asset cadence:**

**Week 1: Research & Outline**
- Pick a topic that answers a real question your ICP asks
- Mine: customer calls, support tickets, sales objections, competitor gaps
- Outline: problem → framework → examples → templates → call to action

**Week 2: Write & Design**
- Write the long-form piece (2,000-5,000 words)
- Include: data, screenshots, templates, specific examples
- Design: visual framework, data visualization, shareable graphics

**Week 3: Repurpose**
- Extract 10-15 social posts (threads, carousels, quotes)
- Newsletter edition (summary + link to full piece)
- 3-5 short-form video scripts if you have video
- Podcast episode outline if you have a podcast

**Week 4: Distribute & Amplify**
- Post socially (native, not link-dump)
- Send newsletter
- Cross-post to relevant communities (Hacker News, Reddit, Slack groups)
- Paid amplification: boost top-performing posts

### Phase 3: Build Distribution Before Creation

**The 80/20 rule: spend 80% on distribution, 20% on creation.**

**Distribution playbook:**
1. **Native social posting.** Don't post links. Post the content natively.
   LinkedIn algorithm punishes external links. X rewards threads. Post the
   full framework or story on-platform, then link in reply or bio.

2. **Community cross-posting.** Find 5-10 communities where your ICP hangs out.
   Don't spam. Be a member first. When you have something genuinely useful,
   post it. Attribution: "Originally shared with my newsletter. Thought this
   group would find it useful too."

3. **Newsletter swaps.** Find 5 newsletters with overlapping audiences.
   Propose: "I'll recommend your newsletter to my list if you recommend mine."
   This is the highest-ROI newsletter growth tactic at $0 cost.

4. **Podcast guest pipeline.** Every podcast appearance = 1,000-10,000 new
   people hearing your name. Pitch 5 podcasts/month. Prepare 3 stories +
   frameworks to share. Always mention your newsletter or free resource.

5. **Paid amplification.** Boost top-performing organic posts (LinkedIn ads,
   X promoted posts). $100-500/month can 10x reach on your best content.

### Phase 4: The Newsletter Engine

**Newsletter as the hub.** Everything else drives to the newsletter.

**Newsletter structure that converts:**
1. **Subject line:** Specific, curiosity-driven, no clickbait
2. **Opening:** 1-2 sentences. Hook them immediately.
3. **Body:** 1 framework or story. Not 3. Not 5. ONE thing.
4. **Proof:** Data, screenshot, example, template
5. **CTA:** Soft (reply to this email) or hard (download template, book demo)

**Growth tactics:**
- Lead magnet in bio: "Free GTM playbook → [link to newsletter signup]"
- End of every social post: "Subscribe for the full breakdown"
- Guest podcast appearances: "Find my newsletter at [URL]"
- Referral program: "Refer 3 friends, get X"

**What NOT to do:**
- Buy email lists (illegal, kills deliverability)
- Spray "subscribe to my newsletter" on every post
- Send inconsistently (weekly is minimum for growth)
- Write essays no one asked for (answer specific questions)

### Phase 5: Measure What Matters

**Content metrics that indicate pipeline, not vanity:**

| Metric | What It Tells You | Target |
|---|---|---|
| Newsletter → Trial/Demo Rate | Are readers becoming buyers? | 1-3% |
| Social → DM Rate | Are followers engaging commercially? | 5-10 DMs/month |
| Content → Inbound Lead Attribution | Which content drives pipeline? | Track with UTMs |
| Newsletter Growth Rate | Is the engine compounding? | 5-10% MoM |
| Podcast Guest → Newsletter Subscribers | ROI of podcast appearances | 50-200 per appearance |
| Time to First Pipeline From Content | When does content start paying? | 3-6 months minimum |

**Attribution model:**
- Self-reported: "How did you hear about us?" on demo form
- UTM parameters: utm_source=newsletter, utm_medium=email, utm_campaign=issue-42
- First-touch: what content did they consume first?
- Multi-touch: what content touched them before converting?

## Common Pitfalls

1. **Creating content nobody asked for.** "What I want to write about" ≠ "what
   my ICP needs to know." Fix: Mine customer calls, support tickets, and sales
   objections for topics. Write what they're already asking about.

2. **Writing but not distributing.** A blog post with no promotion is a tree
   falling in an empty forest. Fix: 80% distribution, 20% creation. For every
   hour writing, spend 4 hours distributing.

3. **Link-dumping on social.** "New blog post: [link]" gets 10% of the reach of
   native content. Fix: Post the full framework natively on social. Link in
   the reply or bio. Give the value on-platform.

4. **Measuring pageviews not pipeline.** 10,000 pageviews that generate 0 demos
   is a hobby, not a GTM channel. Fix: Track content → demo → revenue
   attribution with UTMs and self-reported source.

5. **Too many channels too early.** Newsletter + podcast + YouTube + LinkedIn +
   X + TikTok = burnout in 2 months. Fix: Master ONE channel first. Add
   channels when the first one is producing pipeline consistently.

6. **Inconsistent publishing.** The algorithm rewards consistency. A newsletter
   that goes out "when I have time" never grows. Fix: Same day, same time,
   every week. Pre-write 4 issues before launching.
