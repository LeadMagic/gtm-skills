## Authoritative foundations

### Mark Suster — Invest in Lines, Not Dots
The most important concept in VC outreach. A "dot" = you show up once asking
for money. A "line" = you've been showing progress for 6-12 months before
you ask. VCs invest in lines. Build the line by sending monthly updates to
your target investors BEFORE you're raising.

### Jason Lemkin — How to Pitch VCs
"You don't pitch VCs. You update them on what you've built. The best 'pitch'
is traction. If you have traction, you don't need to pitch."

### Alex Iskold — Cold Email That Works
"The best cold email to a VC is 5 sentences. Any longer and it won't be read.
Lead with traction. Lead with traction. Lead with traction."

## Key reference tables

| Method | Success Rate | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **Warm intro from portfolio founder** | 70-90% | The VC funded them, they vouch for you = golden |
| **Warm intro from respected peer** | 40-60% | Another founder or executive the VC respects |
| **Inbound from demo day / accelerator** | 20-40% | YC, Techstars, etc. VCs attend these looking for deals |
| **Warm intro from service provider** | 15-25% | Lawyer, accountant who works with the VC |
| **AngelList / NFX Signal / other platform** | 5-15% | Some VCs actively source here |
| **Cold email (with traction + personalization)** | 1-5% | Can work if the traction is exceptional |
| **Cold email (generic)** | <1% | Don't bother |
