# VC Outreach

## Overview

VCs receive 5,000+ cold emails per year and take 0.3% of first meetings from
inbound. The mistake: blasting a generic "we're raising" email to 200 funds
and wondering why nobody responds. This skill covers the entire VC outreach
playbook: warm intro strategy, cold email templates that actually work, what
to say in the first meeting, follow-up cadence, and building relationships
6-12 months before you need money. Based on advice from VCs who've funded
thousands of companies.

## Authoritative Foundations

### Mark Suster — Invest in Lines, Not Dots
The most important concept in VC outreach. A "dot" = you show up once asking
for money. A "line" = you've been showing progress for 6-12 months before
you ask. VCs invest in lines. Build the line by sending monthly updates to
your target investors BEFORE you're raising.

### Jason Lemkin — How to Pitch VCs
"You don't pitch VCs. You update them on what you've built. The best 'pitch'
is traction. If you have traction, you don't need to pitch."

### Alex Iskold — Cold Email That Works
"The best cold email to a VC is 5 sentences. Any longer and it won't be read.
Lead with traction. Lead with traction. Lead with traction."

## The Hierarchy of VC Outreach Effectiveness

| Method | Success Rate | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **Warm intro from portfolio founder** | 70-90% | The VC funded them, they vouch for you = golden |
| **Warm intro from respected peer** | 40-60% | Another founder or executive the VC respects |
| **Inbound from demo day / accelerator** | 20-40% | YC, Techstars, etc. VCs attend these looking for deals |
| **Warm intro from service provider** | 15-25% | Lawyer, accountant who works with the VC |
| **AngelList / NFX Signal / other platform** | 5-15% | Some VCs actively source here |
| **Cold email (with traction + personalization)** | 1-5% | Can work if the traction is exceptional |
| **Cold email (generic)** | <1% | Don't bother |

## Step-by-Step Process

### Phase 1: Build the Line (6-12 Months Before Raising)

**Monthly investor updates (send to 10-30 target investors):**

```
Subject: [Company] Update — [Month Year]: [most impressive metric]
```

```
[Company] Monthly Update

Key numbers:
- MRR: $X (↑X% MoM). Customers: X (↑X)
- NRR: X%. Churn: X%
- Burn: $X/mo. Runway: X months

What we shipped:
- [Feature 1]: [impact]
- [Feature 2]: [impact]

What we learned:
- [Insight from customers, market, product]

What's next:
- [Goal for next month]

How you can help (optional):
- [Specific ask — intro, advice, candidate]

Reply rate: just reply to this email. I read every one.

[Jesse — your founder name]
```

**Why this works:**
- When you start raising 6 months later, you're a "line," not a "dot"
- Investors have seen your growth trajectory. They trust the trend.
- You've demonstrated consistency (monthly updates = reliable founder)
- You don't need to "pitch" — the update IS the pitch
- When you send the "we're raising" email, they already know you

### Phase 2: Warm Intro Strategy

**How to get warm intros:**
1. Find the VC on LinkedIn. See who you know in common.
2. Ask your mutual connection: "I see you know [VC name] at [Fund]. We're
   building [one-liner]. Would you feel comfortable making an intro?"
3. Write the intro email FOR your connection — make it one click to forward.

**Warm intro request template (to your connection):**
```
Subject: Intro to [VC name] at [Fund]?

Hi [name],

I'm raising a seed round for [company] — we're at $X ARR, growing X% MoM
with X% NRR. We help [ICP] with [problem].

I noticed you know [VC name] at [Fund]. If you think it's a fit, would you
be comfortable forwarding the intro below? No pressure if not.

Thanks,
[You]

---
Forward below:

[VC name],

[Your name] is the founder of [company] — $X ARR, X% MoM growth, X% NRR.
They help [ICP] solve [problem].

They're raising a [seed/A] round and I thought of you because you focus on
[VC's thesis area]. Worth a conversation.

[Your name]
[Your name's connection]
```

**The golden rule of warm intros:** Make it ONE click for the introducer.
Pre-written forward. Clear context. Low social risk. If they have to write
the intro themselves, most won't.

### Phase 3: Cold Email Templates That Work

#### Cold Email A: The Traction Lead (highest success rate)

```
Subject: [Company] — $X ARR, X% growth, [one-line value prop]

Hi [VC first name],

I'm building [company] — $X ARR, growing X% MoM with X% NRR.

We help [ICP] do [thing] without [pain]. [One sentence on why now].

I'm reaching out because you invested in [portfolio company] and focus on
[VC's thesis area].

I'd love 15 minutes to share what we're seeing and get your perspective.
No fundraising ask — we're building the relationship.

[Your name]
CEO, [Company]
[Link to metrics dashboard or deck]
```

#### Cold Email B: The Insight Lead (when traction is early)

```
Subject: Something we've noticed about [industry/trend]

Hi [VC first name],

We've talked to [X] [ICP] over the past [Y] months, and we keep hearing
the same thing: [insight].

We're building [company] to solve this. We have [early traction — users,
LOIs, pilot commitments].

Your investment in [portfolio company] suggests you see this trend too.

Would love to share what we've learned. 15 minutes?

[Your name]
CEO, [Company]
```

#### Cold Email C: The "You Wrote This" Lead (highest personalization)

```
Subject: Your post on [topic] — we're building it

Hi [VC first name],

Your essay on [specific topic] changed how we think about [area]. This line
stopped me:

"[Exact quote from VC's writing]"

We're building exactly that at [company] — [one sentence on what you do,
connecting to their thesis].

Would you have 15 minutes to tell us if we're on the right track?

[Your name]
CEO, [Company]
```

### Phase 4: The First Meeting

**Your goal:** Get the second meeting. Not to close.

**Structure (30 min):**
1. **Opening (3 min):** "Here's what we do, here's our traction, here's why
   now." No preamble. No "thanks for taking the time."
2. **The story (15 min):** Problem → why existing solutions fail → our approach
   → traction → team → vision. Show product screen, not slides.
3. **Q&A (10 min):** Answer directly. "I don't know" > guessing. Ask them
   questions too — good VCs want to be engaged, not pitched at.
4. **Next step (2 min):** "Based on this, I'd love to continue the conversation.
   What does your process look like from here?"

**What to NEVER say in a first VC meeting:**
- "We're going to be a $10B company" (every founder says this — means nothing)
- "We have no competitors" (means you haven't done research, or there's no market)
- "We'll capture 1% of this $100B market" (top-down TAM = red flag)
- "Our projections are conservative" (they never are)
- "I can't share our numbers until we have an NDA" (VCs don't sign NDAs)
- "We're fundraising — the round is closing fast" (artificial urgency is transparent)

**What to ALWAYS say:**
- Specific traction numbers. Month-by-month. Not "growing fast" — "grew 12% MoM
  for the past 6 months."
- What you've learned from customers. Specific quotes. Specific problems.
- What keeps you up at night. VCs respect honest risk assessment.
- Why YOU are the right team. What unique insight or experience do you have?

### Phase 5: Follow-Up

**After every meeting:**
```
Subject: Thanks — [Company]

Hi [VC name],

Thanks for the conversation today. Key takeaway on my end: [one sentence].

You asked about [topic they asked about] — here's that data: [answer].

We'd love to continue the conversation. Happy to send more detail on [X] or
connect you with [customer/team member].

Best,
[Your name]
```

**The "we're raising" follow-up (after building the line):**
```
Subject: [Company] — raising [Seed/Series A]

Hi [VC name],

I've been sending you monthly updates for the past [X] months. We're now
officially raising our [Seed/A] round.

Quick summary:
- [Key metrics: ARR, growth, NRR, churn]
- Raise: $[X]M on $[Y]M [pre/post]
- Target close: [date]

Would you like to see the deck? Happy to walk through it.

[Your name]
```

### Phase 6: The "Don't Send" List

**Emails that get deleted in under 3 seconds:**

```
❌ "Dear Investor" (not personalized — didn't even use their name)
❌ "We're Uber for X" (lazy framing)
❌ "Revolutionary AI-powered platform" (means nothing)
❌ "Pre-revenue, pre-product, huge vision" (come back when you have something)
❌ "Confidential — please sign NDA" (VCs won't)
❌ "I've CC'd 47 other VCs on this email" (tells each VC they're not special)
❌ 500+ word essays (they won't read it)
❌ "We're raising — the round is closing in 48 hours" (transparently false)
❌ No traction numbers + big claims (zero credibility)
❌ "My co-founder was an E7 at Google" (pedigree without traction = weak signal)
```

## Outreach by Investor Type

### Angel Investors
- **Best approach:** Warm intro or cold email with traction
- **What they care about:** Founder quality, traction, personal connection
- **Email length:** Shortest. 3-4 sentences.
- **Decision speed:** Fastest (days, not weeks)
- **Where to find them:** AngelList, LinkedIn, Twitter, HN, community events

### Seed Funds
- **Best approach:** Warm intro from portfolio founder
- **What they care about:** Traction, team, market, growth rate
- **Email length:** 4-6 sentences with traction numbers
- **Decision speed:** Moderate (2-4 weeks)
- **Where to find them:** Crunchbase, NFX Signal, VC Guide

### Institutional VCs (Series A+)
- **Best approach:** Warm intro from portfolio founder or seed investor
- **What they care about:** Scale, efficiency, team, market size, defensibility
- **Email length:** Can be longer with data, but lead with traction
- **Decision speed:** Slowest (4-8 weeks)
- **Where to find them:** Your existing investors' networks

## Quality Checklist

- [ ] Monthly updates sent to 10-30 target investors for 3+ months before raising
- [ ] Warm intro pre-written for every introducer (one-click forward)
- [ ] Cold emails are under 6 sentences, lead with traction, personalized
- [ ] First meeting structure rehearsed (3-min opening, 15-min story, 10-min Q&A)
- [ ] "Never say" list memorized — no "no competitors," no "Uber for X"
- [ ] Follow-up sent within 2 hours of every meeting
- [ ] Investor CRM tracking every interaction, status, and next step
- [ ] No spray-and-pray ("Dear Investor" → instant delete)

## Common Pitfalls

1. **Raising without building the line.** You show up as a cold "dot" and
   wonder why response rates are 1%. Fix: Start monthly updates 6-12 months
   before raising. Build the line. Show the trend.

2. **Generic cold emails.** "Dear Investor, we're revolutionizing..." is
   deleted in under 3 seconds. Fix: Lead with specific traction. "We're at
   $X ARR growing X% MoM" earns 30 seconds of attention.

3. **Asking for money in the first email.** "We're raising $5M on $30M pre"
   in a cold email = asking a stranger to marry you on the first date. Fix:
   First contact: "I'd love your perspective." Not "I'd love your money."

4. **Spraying 200 VCs simultaneously.** They talk to each other. When 5 VCs
   get the same email and compare notes, you look amateur. Fix: Tier your
   outreach. A-list gets personalized attention. C-list gets batch if needed.

5. **No follow-up after the meeting.** A VC meets 20 founders a week. If you
   don't follow up within 2 hours, they forget which one you were. Fix:
   Follow-up email sent before you leave the parking lot. Every meeting.

6. **Artificial urgency.** "The round is closing fast — we need an answer by
   Friday." This works exactly once: when it's true. VCs have seen it faked
   1,000 times. Fix: Real urgency is traction and competing term sheets, not
   fabricated deadlines.
