# Partner Programs

## Overview
Partners extend your GTM reach without adding headcount. Integration partners embed you in another product's ecosystem. Agency partners sell you into their clients. Co-marketing partners share audiences. This skill designs partner programs by type with clear economics and enablement.

## When to Use
- "Build a partner program"
- "Set up integration partnerships"
- "Create an agency channel"
- "Design co-marketing partnerships"

## Step-by-Step Process
### Phase 1: Partner Type Selection
- Integration: your product works with theirs → mutual customer value
- Agency/Consulting: they implement/sell your product → revenue share or referral fee
- Co-marketing: shared audience → content, webinar, event collaboration
- Technology: complementary tools → joint go-to-market

### Phase 2: Economics
Integration: typically no revenue share (mutual product value). Agency: 15-25% of first-year revenue. Co-marketing: typically no cash exchange (shared audience value).

### Phase 3: Partner Enablement
Onboarding, sales training, joint collateral, deal registration, co-selling support.

## Output Format
Partner program design with partner types, economics, enablement plan, and recruitment strategy.

## Common Pitfalls
1. **Partners without enablement** — partners who cannot sell your product do not sell it.
2. **One-size economics** — different partner types need different models.
3. **No deal registration** — partners need protection on the opportunities they source.
