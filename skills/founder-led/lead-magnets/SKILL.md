# Lead Magnets

## Overview

Most lead magnets don't convert because they're content the creator wanted to
write, not content the prospect desperately needs. The mistake: gating a PDF
that took 2 hours to write and calling it a "lead gen strategy." The best lead
magnets solve an immediate, specific problem so well the prospect would pay for
it — but you give it away free because the LTV of a qualified lead exceeds the
cost. This skill covers magnet ideation, 7 proven formats, build-vs-buy decisions,
landing page optimization, and the nurture-to-pipeline funnel.

## The Cialdini Reciprocity Principle

When you give genuine value first, people feel an obligation to reciprocate.
The average lead magnet conversion rate is 3-5%. An exceptional lead magnet
converts at 15-30%. The difference: perceived value × immediate utility ×
low friction. If someone would screenshot it, forward it to a colleague, or
use it within 5 minutes of downloading — you have a winner.

## 7 Lead Magnet Formats (Ranked by Conversion)

| # | Format | Avg Conv. | Build Time | Best For | Example |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Free Tool / Calculator** | 15-30% | 20-80 hrs | Bottom-funnel, high-intent | ROI calculator, email score grader |
| 2 | **Template / Swipe File** | 10-20% | 5-15 hrs | Practitioners who need to execute | Cold email templates, pitch deck template |
| 3 | **Assessment / Quiz** | 8-15% | 10-30 hrs | Self-discovery, personalization | "What's your GTM maturity score?" |
| 4 | **Benchmark Report** | 5-12% | 20-40 hrs | Data-driven buyers, PR | "SaaS GTM Benchmarks 2026" |
| 5 | **Checklist / Playbook** | 5-10% | 5-10 hrs | Action-oriented, time-pressed | "Pre-Launch Checklist (52 items)" |
| 6 | **Mini-Course (email)** | 3-8% | 10-20 hrs | Education, trust-building | "5-Day Cold Email Mastery" |
| 7 | **Ebook / Whitepaper** | 1-3% | 20-60 hrs | Legacy format — avoid unless uniquely valuable | |

**Rule:** If you can build a tool, build a tool. Tools convert 3-5x better than
PDFs because they deliver value interactively and immediately.

## Step-by-Step Process

### Phase 1: Magnet Ideation

**The ICP pain test — ask 5 questions about every idea:**
1. Does this solve a problem my ICP loses sleep over?
2. Would they pay money for this if I charged? (If no → not valuable enough)
3. Can they consume/use it in under 10 minutes? (If no → too much friction)
4. Is this something they'd forward to a colleague? (If no → not remarkable)
5. Does this naturally lead to our product? (If no → bad lead source)

**Mining ideas from your business:**
- Customer onboarding questions (most common = lead magnet topic)
- Sales objections (answer the objection as a tool/template)
- Support tickets (recurring problems = magnet opportunities)
- Competitor gated content (see what works, make it 10x better)
- Internal processes (your team's playbook = someone's lead magnet)

### Phase 2: Build vs Buy

**When to build custom:**
- Calculator/tool unique to your space (ROI, savings, scoring)
- Template specific to your methodology
- Assessment that feeds your sales process

**When to buy/adapt:**
- Generic calculators (use off-the-shelf + white label)
- Common templates (adapt existing, add your methodology)
- Benchmark reports (partner with a data provider)

**Build cost ranges:**
- Simple calculator (single formula): $2-5K dev, 2-3 weeks
- Interactive tool (multi-step, personalized output): $10-25K, 4-8 weeks
- Assessment/quiz (scoring logic, personalized results): $5-15K, 3-6 weeks
- Templates/swipe files: $0-1K (design), 1-2 weeks

### Phase 3: Landing Page Architecture

**Single-purpose landing page (no navigation, one CTA):**

1. **Hero (above fold):** Headline = the outcome, subhead = what it is, CTA button
2. **Problem agitation:** "You're wasting X hours/$Y on Z" (1-2 sentences)
3. **What you'll get:** 3-5 bullet points, specific ("Not 'improve sales' — '12
   email templates that generated 47% reply rates'")
4. **Social proof:** "Used by X companies" or testimonial or logo bar
5. **The magnet preview:** Screenshot, video walkthrough, or sample output
6. **Form:** Email only (or email + company size). No phone. No address.
7. **CTA:** "Get the calculator" > "Submit" or "Download"
8. **Immediate delivery:** Redirect to magnet or send email instantly

**Form fields rule:** Email only = 15-30% conversion. Email + Name = 10-20%.
Email + Name + Company = 5-10%. Every additional field halves conversion.
Collect more data AFTER they've received value.

### Phase 4: Distribution Strategy

**8 distribution channels (use at least 4):**
1. **Blog posts** — contextual CTAs within relevant articles
2. **Social** — native posts showing the tool/output (not "download my ebook")
3. **Paid ads** — LinkedIn/Google ads driving to landing page
4. **Email signature** — "Free GTM ROI Calculator → [link]"
5. **Podcast appearances** — mention the free tool as the CTA
6. **Partner distribution** — integration partners, agencies promote to their audience
7. **Popups/exit intent** — on high-traffic blog pages (use sparingly)
8. **Newsletter swaps** — trade promotion with complementary newsletters

### Phase 5: Post-Capture Nurture

**Immediate (0-5 minutes after form submission):**
- Deliver the magnet (on-page redirect or instant email)
- "While you explore the [magnet], check out [related case study/blog]"

**Day 1-3: Deepen engagement**
- Email: "How are you using the [magnet]? Here's a pro tip..."
- Email: "Customers who used this [magnet] also found [related resource] useful"

**Day 4-7: Product bridge**
- Email: "The [magnet] shows you X. Our product automates X with [specific feature]."
- CTA: Demo/trial, not "buy now"

**Day 8-14: Social proof close**
- Email: Case study of customer who used magnet → product → result
- CTA: Demo with social proof framing

## Common Pitfalls

1. **Gating something nobody wants.** "Download our company overview PDF" is not
   a lead magnet — it's a brochure. Fix: Would you give YOUR email for this?
   If you hesitate, prospects will bounce.

2. **Too many form fields.** Name + Email + Phone + Company + Role + "How did
   you hear about us?" = 2% conversion. Fix: Email only. Ask for the rest
   during onboarding or via progressive profiling.

3. **Delayed delivery.** "Thanks! We'll email you the magnet within 24 hours."
   50%+ of people never open the email. Fix: Deliver instantly — on-page
   redirect or automated email within 60 seconds.

4. **No nurture sequence.** Capturing an email and doing nothing with it is
   collecting, not converting. Fix: 4-email nurture sequence over 14 days,
   bridging from magnet value → product value → demo CTA.

5. **Magnet disconnected from product.** A lead magnet about "10 best coffee
   shops in Boston" generates leads who like coffee, not your B2B SaaS product.
   Fix: The magnet must naturally bridge to your product's value proposition.

6. **Building instead of testing.** Spending $15K and 6 weeks on a calculator
   before validating demand. Fix: Test with a manual version first (spreadsheet,
   consultant gives you the output). If people use the manual version, build
   the tool.
