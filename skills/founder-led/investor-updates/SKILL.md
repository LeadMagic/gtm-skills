# Investor Updates

## Overview
Investor updates are not status reports — they are the narrative of your company's trajectory. A great update builds confidence, surfaces problems early (when investors can help), and makes fundraising conversations natural rather than desperate. This skill produces structured monthly updates and board decks.

## When to Use
- "Write my monthly investor update"
- "Create a board deck"
- "Prepare fundraising materials"
- "Build an investor narrative"

## Step-by-Step Process
### Phase 1: Monthly Update Structure
1. Headline metric (ARR, growth rate)
2. Wins (what went right)
3. Challenges (what is hard, what help you need)
4. Key metrics dashboard (MRR, churn, CAC, LTV, runway)
5. Asks (specific things investors can help with)
6. Team updates (hires, departures)

### Phase 2: Board Deck
Strategy review, financial performance, product roadmap, team update, key decisions needed.

### Phase 3: Fundraising Narrative
Problem → traction → market → team → ask. Always include: current metrics, growth trajectory, use of funds.

## Output Format
Monthly update template and board deck structure.

## Common Pitfalls
1. **Hiding problems** — investors can help with problems they know about.
2. **Too long** — nobody reads 5-page updates. One page, clear metrics.
3. **No asks** — investors want to help. Give them specific things to do.
