## Two operating principles (Bohrt, Stratyve)

From the [Stratyve interview](https://www.stratyve.com/podcast/sales-development-tito-bohrt):

1. **"SDRs solve problems, not solutions."** Outbound earns a meeting by naming the prospect's problem, not by pitching the product. Pairs with the HockeyStack line: *"Outbound needs a recruiter mindset — you're selling change to someone who's not even looking."*
2. **"Everything gets measured and managed — leading indicators over lagging indicators."** Bohrt's public critique of weak SDR leadership: still asking *"how many dials this week?"* instead of managing the conversion chain.

## Funnel-inversion diagnostic (Bohrt's public method)

Bohrt evaluates an SDR by reading the funnel **bottom-up**, stopping at the first broken step:

```
Meetings completed → Meetings set → Conversations → Dials
```

> "When I evaluate an SDR, the first thing I look at is meetings **completed**… if not there, meetings **set**… if not, **conversations**… if not, **dials**." — Bohrt, Stratyve

The point: **manage outcomes, not inputs.** Activity (dials) is only inspected when an outcome target is missed. Diagnose at the lowest broken rung, then fix with a checklist — not "make more calls."

### Public benchmark bands he manages to

| Conversion step | Bohrt's public benchmark | Diagnostic action if below |
|---|---|---|
| Meeting **set → completed** (show rate) | **80–85%** | Run the show-rate troubleshooting checklist (confirmation cadence, calendar logistics, qualification quality) |
| Opener → pitch (on a live connect) | **~80%+** of connects should turn the opener into a pitch | Coach the opening line via the opener checklist |
| Connect → meeting | Benchmarked across client base (target varies by segment) | Inspect opener, list quality, and persona fit |
| Dials | Effort floor (~**60/day** cited; AltiSales SDR JD lists ~**100 calls/day**) | Only inspected when downstream outcomes are missing |

Worked example he uses: goal **10 meetings/month**; a rep **setting 15** but only **completing 8** is at a **53% show rate** — "that's not going to cut it." Fix the show rate before adding activity. ([Stratyve](https://www.stratyve.com/podcast/sales-development-tito-bohrt))

## SDR economics: the "Dear CFO" math (Bohrt, LinkedIn)

Bohrt's widely-shared ["Dear CFO" post](https://www.linkedin.com/posts/titobohrt_dear-cfo-i-see-your-excel-sheet-showing-activity-7308181719923257345-DiE4) dismantles the naive spreadsheet that prices an SDR as a meeting machine. The strawman model:

```
1 SDR → 14 meetings/mo → 10 opportunities/mo → 3 deals/mo
```

His public objections (each is the actual argument he makes):

1. **Sales-cycle lag is ignored.** With a ~**9-month** sales cycle, the soonest a deal closes is *next year* — so forecasting 10 deals *this* year from a brand-new SDR is fiction. Bookings ≠ revenue-this-period.
2. **No enablement budget.** The model funds a seat but not the **outbound sales deck**, **AE outbound training**, or **RevOps reporting on outbound traction** the motion needs to work.
3. **The comp is too low to attract talent.** *"$50K base, $70K OTE won't bring the right talent"* — and after structure it's *"really ~$60K at most,"* which **disincentivizes good work**.
4. **The output assumption is unrealistic.** *"14 meetings/month without a Data Research team or messaging expertise is pretty crazy."* You can't hit the number without the supporting system.

### The cost-per-meeting formula (how to do it right)

Bohrt's prescription is to compute a **fully-loaded** cost per *qualified* meeting, then extend it through the real funnel and sales-cycle lag — not divide base salary by dialed meetings:

```
Cost per booked meeting =
  (OTE + benefits/payroll tax + manager allocation + tool stack
   + ramp tax + attrition tax)  ÷  qualified meetings HELD

Cost per opportunity = cost per meeting ÷ (show rate × SQL-acceptance rate)
Cost per deal        = cost per opp     ÷ win rate     (then offset by sales-cycle months)
```

**Industry benchmark context (NOT Bohrt's figures — cite separately):** independent 2026 analyses put a fully-loaded US SDR seat at **~2.5–3× OTE**, roughly **$110K–$175K/yr** (some mid-market estimates reach **$200K–$260K** once manager allocation, tooling, ramp and attrition are included), yielding **~$700–$1,100 per booked meeting** in mid-market B2B. Sources: [Azarian Growth Agency — "The SDR Cost Model Is Broken"](https://azariangrowthagency.com/broken-sdr-cost-model/) (citing ICONIQ State of GTM patterns); [aixccelerate — AI vs Human SDR 2026](https://resources.aixccelerate.com/ai-sdr-vs-human-sdr-the-2026-cost-output-and-roi/). Use these as the *industry* anchor; use Bohrt's argument as the *method* (full-loading + sales-cycle reality + enablement budget).

**Talent benchmark (Bohrt, public hiring posts):** a strong AltiSales SDR can earn **$10K+/month in commission**, tracking **$150K–$190K/yr** — evidence that under-comping the role is the false economy. ([LinkedIn](https://www.linkedin.com/posts/titobohrt_we-are-very-close-to-hitting-full-capacity-activity-7210835277647413248-pafL))

## Org design: the assembly line & specialization

AltiSales runs an **assembly-line / specialized** model — the opposite of a generalist SDR doing everything ([AltiSales](https://www.altisales.com/blog/outsourced-sales-development)):

| Role | Owns | Why split |
|---|---|---|
| **Data Research Rep** | Target Market Accounts (TMA), Account Scoring System → ICP, contact data + personalization criteria | Removes the "boring/repetitive" research from the SDR so engagement time is protected |
| **SDR (engagement)** | Multi-channel outreach with pre-built email templates, call scripts, LinkedIn messaging; books & holds meetings | Specialists outperform generalists on the conversation |
| **Tooling (AltiSales.io)** | Analyzes call/email/persona data, tunes messaging toward "message-market fit" | Makes the system data-driven, not rep-dependent |

**Process flow:** TMA → Account Scoring → ICP → Data Research → dedicated SDR + multi-channel plays → data analysis → refine personas/messaging.

**Inbound vs outbound separation.** Bohrt argues outbound ABM/ICP SDRs should **report to Sales**, meet AEs ~1hr / 2 weeks to coordinate multi-threading and revive deals, and act as a **"laboratory"** generating intelligence on subject lines, openers, objections, and which personas respond — data that feeds marketing and AEs. Inbound (speed-to-lead) and outbound are different jobs; don't blend their comp or metrics. ([LinkedIn — should SDRs report to marketing or sales](https://www.linkedin.com/posts/titobohrt_should-sdrs-report-to-marketing-or-sales-activity-7208853474552246272-9eqr))

**Ratios:** Bohrt does not publish a single SDR:AE number; use the WbD POD ratios in `SKILL.md` and the **Bridge Group** industry average of **~1 SDR : 2.5 AE** (varies sharply by ACV/segment; smaller SaaS runs richer SDR coverage). ([Bridge Group via forEntrepreneurs](https://www.forentrepreneurs.com/bridge-group-sales-development/))

**Multi-channel discipline:** phone + email + LinkedIn + (permissioned) text + LinkedIn ABM ads, with channels **feeding each other** — e.g. leave a voicemail that points to an email, then ping the email to the top of the inbox via LinkedIn. ([Stratyve](https://www.stratyve.com/podcast/sales-development-tito-bohrt))

## Hiring & evaluation: craft-first, call-recording-based

What AltiSales does (Bohrt / AltiSales public):

- **Hire proven craft.** Bohrt sources SDRs who are *"masters of the craft"* / *"have done the job before"* — not résumé keyword matches. ([LinkedIn](https://www.linkedin.com/posts/titobohrt_we-are-very-close-to-hitting-full-capacity-activity-7210835277647413248-pafL))
- **Evaluate on recorded reality.** AltiSales managers **listen to 100% of call recordings** for a new SDR's early tenure, then sample ~20% thereafter — coaching is grounded in what actually happened on the phone, not self-report. ([GTMnow](https://gtmnow.com/sdr-ae-handoff/))
- **Outbound skill is the bar.** They explicitly teach reps to talk on the phone and "perfect cold calling" early, treating outbound skill as preparation for future AE/management roles. ([GTMnow](https://gtmnow.com/sdr-ae-handoff/))

**Complementary public method — work-simulation over résumés (30 Minutes to President's Club, *not* Bohrt's framework):** the widely-cited 30MPC hiring method (Nick Cegelski, Armand Farrokh, Mark Kosoglow) is *"hire for traits, train the skills,"* using a **live cold-call roleplay → immediate feedback → second roleplay** to test whether the candidate applies coaching in real time (coachability is the strongest predictor), and *"inverting the funnel"* to sell top candidates. Pair this simulation-based screen with Bohrt's craft-first sourcing and call-recording evaluation. ([30MPC — 4-step interview process](https://www.linkedin.com/posts/30-minutes-to-president-s-club_my-4-step-account-executive-interview-process-activity-7184565947234476033-iDgj))

## SDR ramp & career path

- **Ramp is a system output.** AltiSales markets a "GTM Revamp" that compresses *"three years of development into nine months,"* with dedicated SDRs producing **8–10 qualified meetings/month** once ramped — a function of provided data, scripts, and coaching, not heroics. ([AltiSales](https://www.altisales.com/blog/outsourced-sdr-companies-services))
- **SDR→AE handoff via a binary SLA "system."** Define qualified vs unqualified with **specific, binary** criteria so a *system* (not AE discretion) qualifies the opportunity — removing the AE's incentive to silently disqualify good pipeline. ([GTMnow](https://gtmnow.com/sdr-ae-handoff/))
- **Promotion timing.** Earn AE promotion through demonstrated outbound mastery; **Bridge Group** data shows orgs that wait **≥12 months** before SDR→AE promotion post higher pipeline-productivity scores. ([Bridge Group](https://www.forentrepreneurs.com/bridge-group-sales-development/))

## Where Bohrt disagrees: the AI SDR

Bohrt is a prominent public skeptic of fully-autonomous AI SDRs ([HockeyStack](https://www.hockeystack.com/podcast/where-ai-fails-in-modern-sales-development); [LinkedIn](https://www.linkedin.com/posts/titobohrt_so-a-client-of-mine-has-been-using-an-ai-activity-7242545055536664576-32-v)):

- *"The AI SDR is an oxymoron. As soon as you automate and scale it, it stops working."*
- Client case he cites: an inbound AI SDR booked **23 meetings in 4 months** — the company went from ~**5 meetings/mo to ~5.75**, i.e. **~$10K/mo for ~1 extra meeting**. Verdict: the software-layer price didn't match the marginal output.
- His position: AI can **assist** prep/structure with a **human in the loop**, and a hybrid model helps **inbound time-to-reply**, but "AI-at-scale" replacing the human is not feasible with current models.

Use this to temper any `ai-sdr-setup` recommendation: measure **cost per *incremental* meeting**, not raw AI volume.
