# Bootstrap vs VC Paths

Operating and exit economics by path. **Rob Walling (TinySeed/MicroConf):** optimize ownership and profitable growth. **Jason Lemkin (SaaStr):** venture requires $100M+ credible outcome. **Nathan Latka (GetLatka):** sub-$10M ARR acquisition optionality is real for bootstrapped SaaS.

## Side-by-Side Economics

| Dimension | Bootstrap / TinySeed | Venture (Seed → C+) |
|---|---|---|
| **Ownership at exit** | 70–100% founder/founder-friendly | 15–40% typical after 3–4 rounds |
| **Growth expectation** | 20–50% YoY; profit optional early, required by $2M+ | 2–3x early; 80%+ at scale before efficiency phase |
| **Capital source** | Revenue, TinySeed ($120K–$500K), Founderpath | Seed $2–5M → A $8–20M → B $20–50M+ |
| **Exit types** | MicroAcquire, strategic tuck-in, PE roll-up, hold | Strategic, IPO, PE recap, secondary |
| **Typical exit $** | $3–30M (Latka band); outliers to $100M+ | $100M–$1B+ target; sub-$50M = soft landing risk |
| **Valuation method** | SDE, ARR multiple, profit multiple | ARR multiple + growth premium; DCF for board |
| **CRM / GTM** | HubSpot/Attio until $5M+ | Instrument early; Salesforce + RevOps $5–10M |
| **Hiring trigger** | Cash flow covers 6+ mo runway per hire | Raise ahead of revenue; Lemkin VP Sales ~$2M ARR |

## Bootstrap Paths (Concrete)

### 1. Hold for cash flow (Walling)

- Target: $500K–$5M ARR, 20–40% net margin, 5–15 person team
- Metrics: CAC payback <12 mo, LTV:CAC >3x (Skok), logo churn <2.5%
- Exit: optional; optimize distributions + optionality

### 2. TinySeed / calm raise

- $120K–$500K on founder-friendly terms; not classic VC
- Use for: accelerate without 20% seed dilution
- Still operate bootstrap rules — profitability path by $2M ARR

### 3. Latka-style acquisition optionality

- $1–10M ARR, clean metrics, searchable on GetLatka / MicroAcquire
- Buyers weight: growth rate, churn, founder hours, tech stack
- Prep: `exiting-company/references/due-diligence-metrics-pack.md`

### 4. Bootstrap → raise later

- Build to $1–2M ARR on revenue (increasingly fundable)
- Raise from strength: better terms, less dilution
- Cross-ref: `fundraising-strategy/references/vc-milestone-gates.md`

## VC Paths (Concrete)

### Seed ($500K–$2M ARR)

**Unlock metrics:** PMF signal, 3x YoY or clear acceleration, NRR >100%, magic number >0.5  
**Burn multiple:** >2x acceptable if investing in GTM  
**Gate:** Credible $100M+ ARR path (Lemkin)

### Series A ($1–4M ARR)

**Unlock metrics:** 2–3x YoY, NRR >105–110%, CAC payback <15 mo, burn multiple <2x  
**Gate:** Repeatable sales motion (Roberge machine: hire, train, demand gen, process)

### Series B ($5–15M ARR)

**Unlock metrics:** 2x+ YoY, NRR >110%, magic number >0.75, burn multiple <1.5x  
**Gate:** Category leadership or clear #2 with expansion wedge

### Series C+ / IPO track

**Unlock metrics:** Rule of 40 trending ≥40, burn multiple <1x, GRR >90%  
**Gate:** $100M+ ARR path visible; public-ready governance

## PE Path (Overlap with Bootstrap Exit)

PE buys when bootstrap/late-stage VC company has:

- $5M+ ARR (platform); $20M+ for larger funds
- EBITDA margin 15–25%+ or clear 24-month path
- NRR >110%, GRR >90%, low logo churn
- GTM not founder-dependent

Valuation: **EBITDA multiple (15–25x)** often dominates over raw ARR at scale. See `financial-modeling/references/unit-economics-exit-bridge.md`.

## When Paths Diverge (Decision Rules)

**Choose bootstrap when:**

- Gross margin >70%, organic CAC payback <12 mo without paid blitz
- Market supports $5–20M ARR leader, not winner-take-all
- NRR 100–115% — good business, not venture NRR

**Choose VC when:**

- NRR >120% with land-and-expand in large TAM
- Competitors raising; speed is existential
- 18+ months of burn required before revenue inflects

**Choose PE / sale when:**

- Growth decelerating; standalone DCF < strategic/PE offer
- Founder succession needed; category consolidating
- Multiple inbound strategics (leverage)
