# End Goal Decision Matrix

Use after `gtm-context` and basic unit economics are known. Pick **one primary path**; hold secondary optionality only if it does not split operating focus.

## By ARR Stage

| ARR | Bootstrap default | Venture default | Common exit / liquidity |
|---|---|---|---|
| $0–$500K | Build profitably; founder sells | Raise only if category winner-take-all | None — too early |
| $500K–$2M | Optional TinySeed-style raise; keep control | Seed/Series A if 2x+ growth + large TAM | Acqui-hire rare; focus product-market fit |
| $2M–$10M | Dividends or reinvest; Attio/HubSpot GTM | Series A/B; scale sales | Strategic interest if unique data/IP |
| $10M–$30M | Hold for cash flow; optimize margin | Growth round; international | Secondary possible; strategic LOIs |
| $30M–$100M | Sale or PE if growth slows | Series C+; path to IPO | Banker-led process if inbound |
| $100M+ | Rare — usually PE take-private | IPO or large strategic | IPO, mega-round, strategic |

## Decision Signals

### Choose bootstrap / hold when

- Gross margin > 70% and CAC payback < 12 months without paid blitz
- Founder wants control more than maximum exit $
- Market supports $5–20M ARR leader, not global winner-take-all
- NRR 100–115% — good business, not venture NRR
- Team prefers 10–40 people max

### Choose venture when

- Credible path to $100M+ ARR in category map
- NRR > 120% with land-and-expand
- Competitors raising and buying distribution
- Product requires R&D or GTM spend ahead of revenue for 18+ months
- Founders accept dilution for speed

### Choose strategic sale when

- Acquirer value > standalone DCF (distribution, data, product tuck-in)
- Growth capital requirements exceed appetite
- Founder succession or category consolidation
- Multiple inbound strategics (leverage)

### Choose secondary / partial liquidity when

- Company healthy; founder needs diversification
- Lead investor supports orderly secondary
- No distressed signal to customers or team
- Counsel engaged on rights and pricing

## Anti-Patterns

| Mistake | Why it fails |
|---|---|
| Raise VC without $100M story | Down rounds, founder replacement |
| Bootstrap but copy venture burn | Runway death without follow-on |
| Optimize valuation before retention | Multiples compress on diligence |
| Sell because "offers exist" | Single LOI, bad terms, earnout trap |
| Default Salesforce at $500K ARR | Admin tax; no RevOps owner |

## CRM Implication by Path

| Path | Typical CRM | When to upgrade |
|---|---|---|
| Bootstrap $0–$2M | Attio or HubSpot Starter | HubSpot Pro when marketing automation needed |
| Bootstrap $2M–$10M | HubSpot Pro / Sales Hub | Salesforce when 3+ AEs + complex territories |
| Venture early | HubSpot or Attio + strict data model | Salesforce at $5–10M ARR with RevOps hire |
| Venture scale | Salesforce + CPQ/BI | Dedicated RevOps team |
