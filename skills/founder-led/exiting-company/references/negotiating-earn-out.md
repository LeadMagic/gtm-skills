# Negotiating Earn-Outs — Founder Playbook

**GTM-founder lens:** realistic tradeoffs, what to push for, what to accept, when to walk.  
**Not legal or tax advice.** Binding terms → qualified M&A counsel. Commercial velocity framing → `deal-desk/references/legal-gtm-playbook.md` (Eunice Buhler, Pattern 29).

**Typical ranges cited below are directional — not guaranteed.** Every deal differs by buyer type, sector, and your leverage.

---

## What Earn-Outs Are (and Why Buyers Propose Them)

An **earn-out** defers part of purchase price until post-close performance. Cash at close is lower; founders (often still employed) must hit targets to collect the rest.

Buyers propose earn-outs when:

| Driver | Buyer logic | Founder read |
|---|---|---|
| **Price gap** | LOI EV exceeds what diligence supports | Bridge with performance — or they're buying optionality cheap |
| **Risk transfer** | Churn, concentration, or growth uncertainty | You prove the business; they pay if you deliver |
| **Retention** | Integration needs founder 12–36 months | Compensation for staying — not always bad |
| **Synergy skepticism** | Strategic can't model cross-sell | Revenue milestones prove thesis |

**Sensibility check:** Earn-outs are common in **$3–50M ARR** strategic and PE deals when growth or margin is debated. At **sub-$5M bootstrap sales**, earn-outs are less standard — buyers often prefer clean SDE/ARR deals or walk. If a small buyer insists on a large earn-out with vague metrics, treat as a red flag.

## Structure Types

| Type | Measures | Typical use | Founder risk |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Revenue-based** | ARR or TTM revenue vs baseline | Strategic tuck-ins, PE growth platforms | Buyer can redefine "revenue" (GAAP vs contracted) |
| **EBITDA-based** | Adjusted EBITDA margin | PE majority, bootstrap with profit | Buyer controls OpEx during earn-out — classic trap |
| **Milestone-based** | Product ship, customer win, integration | Acqui-hire, tech bolt-on | Clearer than financials if milestones are objective |
| **Retention / employment-tied** | Stay N months; hit personal KPIs | Founder must run post-close GTM | Overlaps with retention bonus — negotiate which bucket |

**Hybrid deals** (cash + rollover equity + earn-out) are normal at $10M+ EV. **Typical earn-out at risk:** 15–40% of headline EV in mid-market SaaS; 0–25% in clean bootstrap sales. Above **50% at risk** without premium upside or strong audit rights → walk unless strategic necessity.

## Negotiation Levers (Push Here First)

### 1. Measurement period

- **Shorter is better for founders** (12–18 mo vs 36 mo) — buyer integration noise compounds over time.
- Push **annual true-ups** with partial payout vs single cliff at month 36.
- **Acceleration on change of control:** if buyer resells or merges, unpaid earn-out vests or pays at formula — non-negotiable if earn-out >20% of deal.

### 2. Definitions

| Term | Push for | Avoid |
|---|---|---|
| Revenue | Contracted ARR, same methodology as diligence pack | "GAAP revenue" with new rev-rec policy post-close |
| Churn | Logo + dollar churn excluded from penalizing you for buyer-driven changes | Blended metric buyer can worsen via pricing |
| Adjusted EBITDA | Fixed add-back schedule in term sheet | Open-ended "management adjustments" |
| Baseline | Trailing 3-mo ARR at close, locked | Baseline reset if buyer changes pricing/packaging |

Cross-ref metrics definitions → `saas-outcomes/references/exit-metrics-matrix.md`, `due-diligence-metrics-pack.md`.

### 3. Caps, floors, and payout curve

- **Floor:** minimum payout if you stay and business doesn't collapse (e.g. 50% of earn-out if GRR holds >85%).
- **Cap:** buyer may want cap on upside — trade for higher base cash at close.
- **Linear payout** between threshold and max beats cliff-only structures.

### 4. Carve-outs

Founder-friendly carve-outs (get in writing):

- Customer losses from buyer-mandated price increases or sunsetting features
- Headcount or marketing spend cuts imposed by buyer
- Product roadmap changes that delay milestone features
- Key customer (>X% ARR) churn for reasons outside your control

### 5. Governance during earn-out

- **Audit rights** on buyer financials affecting the metric (quarterly minimum)
- **Operating covenant:** no unilateral changes to pricing, comp plans, or GTM budget beyond ±X% without earn-out committee review
- **Dispute resolution:** independent accountant tie-break (not "buyer CFO is final")

Handoff for contract language → `deal-desk/references/legal-gtm-playbook.md` + counsel.

## Founder Sensibility: Fair vs Trap

### When earn-out is fair

- Gap is **real** (diligence found concentration or growth deceleration) and earn-out replaces a price cut you'd accept anyway
- Metrics match **metrics you already run** (NRR, ARR bridge, cohort retention)
- You **want** to stay 18–24 months and buyer cash at close covers life + tax planning
- **Rollover equity** aligns long-term upside — earn-out is incremental, not sole upside

### When earn-out is a trap

- **>40% at risk** with buyer-controlled spend and no carve-outs
- Vague metrics ("customer satisfaction", "strategic synergies")
- **Employment at-will** + earn-out forfeiture on termination without cause protection
- Buyer won't share **segment-level** reporting during measurement
- Headline multiple **requires** earn-out max to match LOI — they never intended full cash price

### Walk-away thresholds (minimum acceptable)

Define before LOI (see `templates/earn-out-term-sheet-review.md`):

| Threshold | Example floor | Walk if |
|---|---|---|
| Cash at close | ≥60% of minimum acceptable after-tax need | <50% without strong rollover |
| Earn-out at risk | ≤35% of EV unless strategic premium | >50% with weak definitions |
| Employment term | ≤24 mo with good-cause protection | Unlimited at-will + full forfeiture |
| Metric control | Audit + carve-outs | Buyer refuses both |

Model after-tax scenarios → `financial-modeling` + `templates/valuation-sensitivity-table.md`.

### Tax timing (high level only)

- Earn-out payments often taxed as **ordinary income** when received (not at close) — increases planning gap vs lump-sum capital treatment in some structures.
- **Consult CPA** before signing — structure (asset vs stock sale, installment treatment) changes net proceeds materially.
- Do not optimize headline EV without after-tax, time-value-of-money comparison.

## Earn-Out vs Retention Bonus vs Rollover

| Mechanism | Purpose | Founder preference |
|---|---|---|
| **Earn-out** | Pay for business performance | OK if metrics are objective and audited |
| **Retention bonus** | Pay for staying (time-based) | Prefer separate from performance — simpler |
| **Rollover equity** | Align on upside in NewCo | Good for strategic; understand liquidation prefs |
| **Seller note** | Buyer defers cash; you lend | Only with security, interest ≥ market, subordination clear |

**Sensibility:** Ask buyer to **split** retention (time) from performance (earn-out). Mixing "stay and hit ARR" into one tranche without carve-outs is how founders lose both jobs and upside.

## Anti-Patterns

| Anti-pattern | Why it hurts | Fix |
|---|---|---|
| Vague metrics | Buyer interprets against you | Lock formulas + worked examples in term sheet |
| Buyer controls spend during earn-out | They can make EBITDA impossible | OpEx covenant or EBITDA carve-outs |
| No audit rights | You can't verify calculations | Quarterly audit + dispute accountant |
| Full forfeiture on departure | Fired month 11, lose everything | Good-cause / double-trigger partial vest |
| Competing with LOI headline | " $20M deal" = $12M cash + $8M earn-out max | Model **cash at close** in all materials |

## Decision Tree

```
LOI includes earn-out?
├─ No → Still model hold vs sell; confirm no hidden retention clawbacks
└─ Yes → What % of EV at risk?
    ├─ <25% + clear metrics + audit → Negotiate carve-outs; likely acceptable if cash at close OK
    ├─ 25–40% → Require acceleration, EBITDA/revenue definitions, employment protection
    └─ >40% → Re-trade for cash, rollover, or walk
         └─ Strategic must-have? → Independent counsel + earn-out committee before signing
```

## Checklist: Earn-Out Term Sheet Review

Use `templates/earn-out-term-sheet-review.md` as the worksheet. Minimum review:

- [ ] Cash at close ≥ personal floor (after tax, debt paydown)
- [ ] Earn-out % of EV documented; matches LOI narrative
- [ ] Metric definition matches diligence ARR bridge methodology
- [ ] Measurement period ≤24 mo (or accelerated payouts)
- [ ] Carve-outs for buyer-imposed changes
- [ ] Audit rights and reporting cadence
- [ ] Change-of-control acceleration
- [ ] Employment terms separated from performance forfeiture
- [ ] Dispute mechanism (not buyer-only final say)
- [ ] CPA review on timing and structure
