# Adam Robinson — Founder-Led LinkedIn Playbook (RB2B / Retention.com)

Reference for `SKILL.md`. Adam Robinson is the bootstrapped founder/CEO of
Retention.com (ecommerce visitor identity, ~$22M ARR with a single-digit team)
and RB2B (person-level B2B website visitor identification). His companies run
a combined ~$25M+ ARR with LinkedIn as effectively the only distribution
channel. RB2B went $0 → $1M ARR in 16 weeks and ~$5M ARR in its first
year with a team of ~4-5 — no paid ads, no outbound team, one founder
posting daily.

## The playbook

### 1. Content-market fit before content volume

Robinson's biggest documented lesson: his build-in-public posts at
Retention.com built a loyal audience of **SaaS founders who couldn't buy his
DTC product**. The content worked; the audience-product match didn't. He
launched RB2B — the B2B version of the same product — so the audience he had
already built became the ICP. Check audience-ICP match *before* scaling
content effort. (He calls this content-market fit, by analogy to PMF.)

### 2. Three to four content pillars, repeated forever

Build in public (real revenue, churn, hiring, pivots) · the future of GTM ·
bootstrapping vs VC · founder psychology. He posts variations on the same
pillars over and over — repetition builds topic authority (see
`linkedin-algorithm` interest-graph mechanics).

### 3. Hook discipline — 25% of writing time

He won't publish a post without a strong opening line. Hook checklist:

- Did you maximize professional credibility ("Bootstrapped 3 companies to…")?
- Did you use numbers and data?
- Did you say something provocative?
- Is it in your "prolific zone" (where you have earned authority)?
- Are you brokering valuable information the reader can't easily get?

If you haven't done the thing yourself, borrow credibility by curating
others' results (he calls this the "Oprah framework").

### 4. Post as if nobody knows you

Only ~15% of your audience overlaps between posts (~85% of viewers didn't see
the last one). Every post must stand alone: reintroduce context, credibility,
and stakes each time. Optimizing for narrative continuity across posts is a
mistake.

### 5. Zero-click content; the profile link sells

Give the full insight inside the post — no teaser links, no gated PDFs.
LinkedIn rewards it (external links are reach-penalized) and readers trust
it. Conversion happens through the profile "visit my website" link and warm
DMs; nearly all RB2B traffic arrives that way. Roughly 1 in 10 posts carries
a hard CTA.

### 6. Radical transparency as differentiation

Revenue, churn, failed launches, competitive fights — posted with real
numbers. The famous example: he turned a competitor's cease-and-desist letter
into a viral post (hundreds of thousands of impressions, ~1,600 qualified
leads through a simple Google Form). Vulnerability + numbers + repetition
beat polished PR.

### 7. Engagement loop around the posts

- Reply to every comment (golden-hour discipline → `linkedin-algorithm`).
- Send up to ~10 connection requests/day to ICP profiles.
- Message new connections ~60 days after they accept — warm, not pitch-first.
- Host or guest on podcasts to compound authority between posts.

### 8. Amplify winners with thought leadership ads

Test organically; put ad spend only behind posts that already proved demand.
Organic is the A/B test, paid is the multiplier (see `paid-social-strategy`).
