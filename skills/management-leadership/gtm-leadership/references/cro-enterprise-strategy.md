## McMahon — enterprise sales leadership principles

### 1. MEDDICC as operating system (not paperwork)

McMahon created the MEDDICC spine at PTC; Force Management and Andy Whyte operationalized scorecards. Shared language across product, marketing, finance, and board.

| Letter | CRO inspection question |
|---|---|
| **M**etrics | Quantified business case documented? |
| **E**conomic Buyer | EB engaged with mutual plan? |
| **D**ecision Criteria | Written and validated? |
| **D**ecision Process | Steps, dates, approvers mapped? |
| **I**dentify Pain | Buyer-stated pain — not rep narrative? |
| **C**hampion | Insider who wins if deal closes — armed to sell? |
| **C**ompetition | Alternatives including status quo scored? |

**Cross-links:** `pipeline-management` (MEDDICC gates) · `meeting-prep` · `sales-coaching` · Gap Selling anti–happy ears (`keenan-gap-selling.md`)

### 2. Inspect what you expect — weekly cadence

McMahon's thesis: forecasts stabilize when leaders **coach deals through qualification**, not when reps report confidence.

| Cadence | Focus | McMahon rule |
|---|---|---|
| **Weekly 1:1** | 2–3 deals deep — MEDDICC proof per stage | Role-play slipping deals; no happy ears |
| **Weekly team** | Pipeline coverage, new logos, slipping commit | Common language on qualification |
| **Monthly** | Productivity per rep, ramp cohort, territory health | Headcount is not a strategy |
| **Quarterly** | Territory redesign, quota true-up, ICP evolution | Fair territories = retention |

### 3. Hire A players; coach B players; exit C players fast

- **A players:** Self-sourcing pipeline, methodology discipline, make peers better
- **Interview:** Backchannel refs from direct reports (McMahon + Roberge overlap)
- **Ramp:** ~**6 months** to full quota productivity for enterprise AE (Logan Bartlett / McMahon) — reconcile with Bridge 4.5–6 mo in `benchmark-reconciliation.md`
- **Managers:** Hire managers **before** the rep wave — "build the road before driving 50 reps across the bridge" (McMahon metaphor)

### 4. Board reporting — RevOps as strategic eyes

From RevOps Lab interview with McMahon:

| Board view | Contents |
|---|---|
| **5-quarter model** | Historical + current + 3 forward quarters |
| **Productivity per rep** | Bookings or consumption ÷ quota-carrying headcount |
| **New logos vs expansion** | Mix shift explicit — consumption cos weight expansion |
| **Three-view forecast** | Commit / likely / upside — each with MEDDICC proof |
| **Risk mitigations** | Named pull-forward deals if commit slips |

**Cross-link:** `gtm-metrics` → `public-company-gtm-metrics.md` · Meritech · Henry Schuck earnings discipline

## Snowflake — consumption + expansion GTM

**Public operators:** Frank Slootman (CEO, *Amp It Up*) · Chris Degnan (early GTM leader, GTMnow interview) · Mike Scarpelli (CFO — capital discipline, efficiency narrative in earnings)

### Consumption alignment (Slootman)

Snowflake recognized revenue on **consumption**, but early GTM paid reps on **bookings** — misalignment caused oversell, downsells, and commission cost out of sync with revenue.

| Shift | Before | After |
|---|---|---|
| Rep incentive | Bookings / TCV | Consumption + successful deployment |
| Forecast lens | Contract value | Usage run-rate, capacity reorder |
| Post-sale | Optional CS handoff | Rep accountable for activation + expansion |
| Executive comp | Siloed MBOs | Unified metrics across exec team |

**Expansion implication:** Land deals were often **paid pilots** ($40–60K ACV segments per Degnan) — one SKU (Snowflake credit), then **use-case expansion** tracked in CRM. Reps paid on booking **and** consumption → motivated to drive usage, not hit-and-run.

**Cross-link:** `expansion-selling` (consumption triggers) · `executive-compensation` (consumption gates)

### Execution culture (Slootman)

- **Amp It Up:** Raise standards, narrow focus, increase urgency — not "do more with less" theater
- **Industry GTM:** Mid-growth pivot to financial services, healthcare, media pods — ecosystem + SI alignment
- **Professional services** over large CS bureaucracy for deployment (Slootman view) — product/engineering own depth adoption

**Anti-pattern:** Treating Snowflake's natural consumption PMF as universal — most SaaS must build CS/expansion motions deliberately (see Gainsight critique of Slootman CS take).

## Databricks — open-source funnel to enterprise

**Public operators:** Ali Ghodsi (CEO) · Ron Gabrisko (CRO)

### PLG + sales — complementary, not exclusive

Ghodsi (Battery Ventures podcast): PLG works early; **enterprise sales force required to scale**. Engineering pressure + sales pressure together improved the product — "kick-ass sales team" + "awesome product teams."

| Layer | Job | Databricks pattern |
|---|---|---|
| **PLG / community** | Developer adoption, Apache Spark community, trials | Top-of-funnel awareness and experimentation |
| **Inside sales** | Tech startups, fast cycles, bullpen energy | Early $1M ARR motion |
| **Field enterprise** | Fortune 500, financial services | Gabrisko hired **40 enterprise reps in Q1** when pipeline justified |
| **Consumption (DBU)** | Align price to data/query growth | Same mental model as cloud infra buyers (AWS/Azure) |

### Technical sales org

- Sales leaders with **engineering depth** — run POCs, not slide decks (Gabrisko, SaaStr)
- **Investor channel:** a16z intros to CIOs — underused enterprise pipeline source
- **Hybrid routing:** High-velocity inside for volume; field for large ACV — "do both if both are profitable"

### Handoff: product usage → enterprise

1. Open-source / trial usage creates **propensity signals**
2. Sales reaches accounts showing **buying intent** (not cold PLG users)
3. Land with consumption pricing; expand with workloads and use cases
4. Algorithmic growth (recent Databricks public commentary): propensity scores allocate solution architects and senior sellers

**Cross-link:** `freemium-optimization` · `product-led-growth` skills · Pattern 31

## Field sales vs inside vs PLG — decision matrix

| Signal | Motion | Example |
|---|---|---|
| ACV <$25K, short cycle, high volume | Inside sales + PLG assist | Databricks early startup segment |
| ACV $25–100K, multi-stakeholder | Inside or hybrid POD | Standard B2B SaaS |
| ACV $100K+, regulated, field required | Field + SE/SA technical | Snowflake enterprise, Databricks F500 |
| Developer bottom-up adoption | PLG acquisition → sales expansion | Databricks, early MongoDB |
| Usage-based revenue | Consumption comp + CS/expansion tie-in | Snowflake, Databricks DBU |

**McMahon rule:** Do not add field reps until **MEDDICC inspection** works at inside layer — scaling broken qualification multiplies fiction.

## Anti-patterns

| Anti-pattern | Symptom | Fix |
|---|---|---|
| **Happy ears** | Pipeline inflates; commits slip | MEDDICC + Gap Selling exit criteria; McMahon deal-coaching role plays |
| **Lone wolf AEs** | Top rep hides process; team can't replicate | Mandatory CRM methodology; inspection on deal evidence |
| **Premature scale** | Hire 50 reps before managers/process | McMahon bridge metaphor — managers + process first |
| **Bookings-only comp** (consumption cos) | Oversell, downsell, churn | Slootman alignment — pay on usage + deployment |
| **PLG denial** | Sales team ignores product signals | Databricks hybrid — PLG feeds propensity |
| **PLG-only delusion** | No enterprise sales until too late | Ghodsi — add enterprise sales before growth stalls |
| **Territory lottery** | Rep churn from unfair patches | Quarterly territory true-up (McMahon + Force Management) |

## Checklists

### CRO first 90 days

| Week | Actions |
|---|---|
| 1–2 | Audit forecast accuracy (last 4 quarters); MEDDICC field coverage in CRM |
| 3–4 | Weekly inspection cadence live; identify top 10 slipping commits |
| 5–6 | Territory fairness review; manager span of control (6–8 ICs) |
| 7–8 | Comp plan audit — bookings vs consumption vs NRR alignment |
| 9–12 | Board 5-quarter model draft; hiring plan vs productivity curve |

### QBR inspection cadence (quarterly)

- [ ] Quota attainment **distribution** (not hero rep story)
- [ ] New logo vs expansion mix vs plan
- [ ] Ramp cohort productivity (month 3 / 6 / 9)
- [ ] Win/loss by competitor and by ICP segment
- [ ] Pipeline coverage by segment (3× minimum for enterprise)
- [ ] MEDDICC average score on commit bucket
- [ ] Territory changes documented with effective date
- [ ] S&M efficiency trend (Meritech / public metrics if scale stage)
