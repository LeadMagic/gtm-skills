# V2MOM — Marc Benioff Goal-Alignment System

Salesforce runs on **V2MOM** — one document per leader, per year, visible to the
org. Marc Benioff introduced it to align thousands of employees; revenue teams
at any size benefit from the same clarity.

**Source:** Marc Benioff, *Behind the Cloud*; [Salesforce V2MOM history](https://www.salesforce.com/news/stories/v2mom-salesforce-planning-methodology/)

---

## Why V2MOM beats generic OKRs for GTM

| OKR problem | V2MOM fix |
|---|---|
| Objectives without values → bad behavior | **Values** constrain how goals are hit |
| Key results without methods | **Methods** name the actual plays |
| Surprises in Q3 | **Obstacles** surface blockers in January |
| Vanity metrics | **Measures** tie to revenue outcomes |

Use V2MOM for **annual** GTM strategy; use quarterly OKRs for **execution sprints**
if you need both — V2MOM is the constitution, OKRs are the quarterly amendments.

---

## Each section — how to write it

### Vision (1–2 sentences)

Answer: *If we succeed this year, what is true?*

- Good: "Every mid-market fintech in NA that buys CRM evaluates us in the top 3."
- Bad: "Grow revenue." (Not a vision — it's a measure)

### Values (3–7 bullets)

Behaviors you will **not sacrifice** for the number.

Examples from Benioff's public framing:

- Trust with customers
- Innovation that customers can adopt
- Equality and stakeholder success (adapt to your company)

GTM-specific values:

- "Forecast honesty over sandbagging"
- "Customer success before expansion pitch"
- "No brilliant jerks" (Patty McCord alignment)

### Methods (5–10 prioritized actions)

The **how** — ordered list, not a wish list.

| Priority | Method | Owner |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Launch outbound POD (2 SDR : 1 AE) | VP Sales |
| 2 | CRM hygiene program — 90% required fields | RevOps |
| 3 | CS-led expansion playbook on top 50 accounts | Head of CS |

### Obstacles (honest list)

What will stop you if ignored.

- "No enrichment budget — lists decay"
- "AE ramp 6 months — capacity gap H1"
- "Single-threaded enterprise deals — no EB access"

**Rule:** Every obstacle gets a Method or Measure response — or deprioritize the vision.

### Measures (5–8 numbers)

Quantitative proof the Methods worked.

| Measure | Baseline | Target | Cadence |
|---|---:|---:|---|
| ARR | $4.2M | $6.5M | Monthly |
| NRR | 108% | 115% | Quarterly |
| SQO → Opp conversion | 18% | 25% | Monthly |
| Forecast accuracy | ±22% | ±10% | Quarterly |
| CRM hygiene score | 72% | 90% | Weekly |

## Cascading V2MOM (revenue org)

```
Company V2MOM (CEO)
└── GTM V2MOM (CRO / VP Sales)
    ├── Sales V2MOM (manager per team)
    ├── Marketing V2MOM (pipeline + brand measures)
    └── CS V2MOM (NRR, GRR, adoption measures)
```

**Individual rep quotas** should trace to **Measures** — if a rep's number does not
connect to a Method, the plan is arbitrary.

---

## Cadence

| When | Action |
|---|---|
| **December / January** | Draft company + GTM V2MOM |
| **Week 2** | Team review — challenge Obstacles |
| **Monthly** | Score Measures; adjust Methods |
| **Quarterly** | Public readout to company (Benioff tradition) |
| **Annual** | Archive; write fresh — do not edit in place all year |

---

## Common mistakes

1. **Methods = task list.** "Send more emails" is not a method — "Outbound POD with ICP fintech" is.
2. **Values poster only.** If comp plan rewards churn-and-burn, Values are fiction.
3. **No Obstacles.** Teams that skip this section repeat the same blockers annually.
4. **50 measures.** Pick 5–8 that the CRO reviews every month.
5. **V2MOM in a drawer.** Publish internally (Notion, all-hands slide) — Benioff made V2MOM visible.
