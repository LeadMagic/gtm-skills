# Resignation Playbook — Voluntary Departures

*Expert anchors: **Kim Scott** (Radical Candor — care on exit), **Jason Lemkin** (SaaStr — pipeline handoff speed), **Mark Roberge** (documented systems), **Patty McCord** (no guilt retention theater). Legal steps → `employment-compliance`.*

## Voluntary vs involuntary

| Type | Trigger | This playbook |
|---|---|---|
| **Resignation** | Employee gives notice | Yes |
| **Termination** | Company ends employment | `templates/termination-conversation.md` |
| **Mutual separation** | Negotiated exit | HR + legal |

## Employee side — resigning professionally

### Before you resign

- [ ] Written offer accepted elsewhere (or decision final)
- [ ] Review employment agreement: notice period, non-compete, IP, commission clawback
- [ ] Plan customer handoff — do not ghost accounts
- [ ] Export personal contacts you own; do not take company CRM data

### Resignation conversation (manager first)

**Structure:** Short, direct, grateful — no ambush by email.

> "I've accepted another role / decided to pursue a new opportunity. My last day will be [date per policy]. I want to make handoff as smooth as possible."

**Do not:** Vent grievances in the resignation meeting — use exit interview or skip.

### Resignation email (HR + manager)

```
Subject: Resignation — [Your name], last day [date]

Hi [Manager] and [HR],

I'm resigning from my role as [title], effective [last day].

Thank you for [specific — one sentence]. I'll document open opps and complete handoff per the checklist.

[Name]
```

## Manager side — receiving resignation

### First 15 minutes (Kim Scott: care personally)

1. **Thank them** — "I appreciate you telling me directly."
2. **Ask why** (once) — listen; don't debate or guilt-trip
3. **Confirm last day** — per policy; negotiate only if critical deal cycle
4. **Assign handoff owner** — manager + buddy; CRM same-day update
5. **No counter-offer reflex** — see counter-offer policy below

Script: `templates/resignation-conversation-script.md`

### Same day

| Action | Owner | SLA |
|---|---|---|
| CRM: reassign all opps | Manager | <24h |
| Calendar: remove from customer meetings | Rep + manager | Same day |
| Slack / email access plan | IT | Per policy |
| Team communication | Manager | Same day — factual, no gossip |
| Customer intro to new owner | Manager | <48h on active deals |

### Counter-offer policy

| Situation | Recommended response |
|---|---|
| Top performer, fixable issue (comp, territory) | Counter only if you would offer **without** resignation threat |
| Culture / manager issue | Counter rarely works — they leave in 6 months |
| Leaving for stage/role growth | Congratulate; boomerang policy |
| Performance concern | Do not counter |

**Lemkin rule:** If you wouldn't change comp/role tomorrow unprompted, don't counter today.

## Exit interview

**Purpose:** Learn — not retain. HR or skip-level runs; manager does not lead exit interview.

Questions: `templates/exit-interview-questions.md`

| Do | Don't |
|---|---|
| Ask about systems, manager support, comp clarity | Argue with feedback |
| Aggregate themes quarterly | Share identifiable quotes with manager without HR |
| Thank them | Promise reference you won't give |

## Boomerang / alumni policy

Document whether rehire is welcome:

| Exit type | Typical policy |
|---|---|
| Good performer, growth leave | Welcome back in 12+ months |
| Performance exit | No rehire |
| Toxic behavior | No rehire |

## Resignation pitfalls

1. **Email-only resignation.** Feels cowardly; damages references. Fix: conversation first.
2. **Immediate counter without diagnosis.** Buys 90 days. Fix: root-cause retro if you counter.
3. **Manager takes resignation personally.** Team watches. Fix: professional handoff + team comm.
4. **No pipeline reassignment.** Revenue dies. Fix: 24h CRM rule.
5. **Skip exit interview.** Repeat attrition. Fix: quarterly theme review.
