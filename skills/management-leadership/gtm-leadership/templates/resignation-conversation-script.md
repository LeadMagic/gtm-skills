# Resignation Conversation — Manager Script

**Employee:** [Name] · **Role:** [Title] · **Date:**

*Kim Scott — care personally, don't ruinous-empathy retain someone who decided.*

---

## When they tell you

**Listen fully.** Do not interrupt with counter-offer.

> "Thank you for telling me directly. I want to understand so we can support a smooth transition."

**Optional — one clarifying question:**
> "What's the main factor in your decision?"

*Listen. Do not argue, guilt, or list why they're wrong.*

---

## Confirm logistics

> "What last day are you targeting? Our policy is [X weeks]. Does [date] work for handoff?"

> "Here's what happens next: we'll reassign accounts in CRM today, [HR] will send offboarding steps, and I'll communicate to the team this afternoon."

---

## If you consider a counter-offer

**Pause.** Only proceed if you would make the change without resignation.

> "I'd like to understand if there's something we could have changed earlier. I'm not going to pressure you — if you've decided, we respect that."

If countering (rare):
> "Would you be open to a follow-up conversation in 48 hours after you've had time to think? I'd like to discuss [specific: territory, OTE, title path] — only if that's relevant to your decision."

**Do not:** Match offer blindly; create precedent for resign-to-negotiate.

---

## Close with dignity

> "I appreciate what you've contributed — especially [specific win]. Let's make the next [X] days a clean handoff for customers and the team."

---

## Same-day manager checklist

- [ ] CRM opps reassigned
- [ ] Handoff doc requested (`resignation-handoff-checklist.md`)
- [ ] Team comm sent (no drama)
- [ ] HR notified in writing
- [ ] Customer transitions scheduled on active deals
- [ ] Exit interview scheduled (HR) — optional for employee
