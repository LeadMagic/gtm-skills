# Exit Interview Questions

**Employee:** [Name] · **Role:** [Title] · **Last day:** [Date]  
**Interviewer:** [HR / skip-level — not direct manager]  
**Framework:** Learn systems failures; Kim Scott listen mode; aggregate themes only.

---

## Opening

> "Thanks for doing this. Nothing here goes to your manager as quoted feedback — we aggregate themes. You can skip any question."

---

## Why leaving

1. What was the primary reason you started looking for another role?
2. What was the moment you decided to accept the other offer?
3. If you could change one thing about this role, what would it be?

*Listen — do not defend company or manager.*

---

## Role & enablement

4. Did you have clear quota, territory, and success criteria in your first 90 days?
5. Did you get the tools, lists, and coaching you needed to ramp?
6. How would you rate onboarding on a 1–10? What was missing?

Cross-ref: `revenue-team-onboarding` → onboarding-questions for gap analysis.

---

## Management & culture

7. Did you receive honest, timely feedback on performance?
8. Did you feel safe raising blockers with your manager?
9. Would you recommend this company to a friend in GTM? Why or why not?

---

## Comp & growth

10. Was compensation (OTE, quota attainability, equity) aligned with expectations set at hire?
11. Did you see a credible path to the next role here?
12. Did anything about comp or promotion feel opaque or unfair?

---

## Systems

13. What slowed you down most — CRM, process, marketing support, product gaps?
14. What should we keep doing that worked well?

---

## Close

15. Anything else we should hear?

> "Thank you. We wish you well. Alumni network / reference policy: [state policy]."
