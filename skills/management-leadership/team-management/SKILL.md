# Team Management

## Overview

Management is a skill, not a promotion. Most founders become managers by
default — they hire someone and now they have to manage them. The mistake:
treating management as "telling people what to do" instead of building a
system that enables autonomous, high-quality work. This skill covers the
core management practices every founder needs: 1:1s, feedback, goal-setting,
delegation, and scaling yourself as a leader. Based on the operators who
built the best-managed companies in tech.

## Step-by-Step Process

### Phase 1: 1:1s (The Foundation of Management)

**Cadence:** Weekly. 30 minutes. Same day/time every week. Never cancel. If
you cancel, you're saying "you're not a priority."

**Agenda (owned by the report, not the manager):**
1. What's going well? (5 min)
2. What's challenging / blocking? (10 min — the most important part)
3. What do you need from me? (5 min)
4. Career / growth discussion (5 min — at least monthly)
5. Feedback (5 min — both directions, every time)

**Manager's role in 1:1s:** Listen 80%. Ask questions. Don't solve problems
immediately — ask "what do you think we should do?" first. Take notes. Follow
up on action items from last 1:1.

### Phase 2: Feedback (Continuous, Not Annual)

**Radical Candor framework (Kim Scott):**
| | Challenge Directly | Don't Challenge |
|---|---|---|
| **Care Personally** | RADICAL CANDOR | Ruinous Empathy |
| **Don't Care** | Obnoxious Aggression | Manipulative Insincerity |

**Feedback formula:** "When you [specific behavior], the impact was [specific
result]. In the future, I'd suggest [specific change]." Example: "When you
presented the metrics without context, the board spent 20 minutes confused.
In the future, include a one-sentence summary of what each number means."

### Phase 3: Goal Setting (OKRs)

Objective: Where do we want to go? (qualitative, inspiring)
Key Results: How do we know we're getting there? (quantitative, 3-5 per objective)

**OKR cadence:** Set quarterly. Check weekly. Grade at end of quarter.
Target: 70% achievement (if you're hitting 100%, you're sandbagging).

### Phase 4: Delegation (Molly Graham — Giving Away Your Legos)

**The delegation ladder:** Level 1: I do, you watch. Level 2: You do, I watch.
Level 3: You do, I review. Level 4: You do, you own. Level 5: You teach someone else.

**What to delegate:** Anything someone else can do 80% as well as you. Your job
is to work on things ONLY you can do.

### Phase 5: Remote Team Management

**Asynchronous-first:** Default to async. Meetings are for decisions, not status.
**Written culture:** Write everything down. Decisions, context, plans.
**Overcommunicate:** In an office, people overhear. Remote doesn't. Say it twice.

## Quality Checklist

- [ ] Weekly 1:1s with every direct report (30 min, never canceled)
- [ ] Feedback given in the moment (not saved for review cycle)
- [ ] Quarterly OKRs set with 3-5 KRs per objective
- [ ] Delegation ladder used for every new responsibility
- [ ] Team rituals documented (standup, retro, planning, all-hands)

## Common Pitfalls

1. **Canceling 1:1s.** "Nothing to discuss this week" = you're not asking the
   right questions. Fix: Never cancel. Agenda belongs to the report.
2. **Saving feedback for review.** 6 months of saved feedback = "why didn't
   you tell me this in January?" Fix: Real-time feedback. Radical Candor.
3. **Doing instead of delegating.** "It's faster if I just do it." True today.
   False tomorrow. Fix: Delegation ladder. 80% as good is good enough.
