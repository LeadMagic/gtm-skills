# Team Management

## Overview
Managing a GTM team requires a deliberate operating system — not intuition.
This skill covers hiring for cultural contribution, structured onboarding
(30/60/90 day), performance management that is fair and transparent, and
team rhythms that create predictability without bureaucracy.

## When to Use
- "Set up team management"
- "Build a rep onboarding program"
- "Design team operating cadence"
- "Handle performance issues"
- "Create team culture"

## Authoritative Foundations
WbD GTM Operating Model: management is a system, not an art. Predictable
outcomes come from predictable processes. Force Management leadership
principles: managers inspect what they expect. Culture is what you tolerate.

## Step-by-Step Process
### Phase 1: Hiring
Hire for three dimensions: competence (can they do the job), character
(will they do the right thing), and cultural contribution (will they make
the team better). Score candidates against all three.

### Phase 2: Onboarding (30/60/90)
30 days: learn the product, shadow calls, understand ICP. 60 days: own
pipeline, run discovery independently. 90 days: full ramp, quota-bearing.
Document the playbook before hiring.

### Phase 3: Operating Cadence
Daily standup (15 min), weekly team meeting (60 min), monthly all-hands
(60 min), quarterly planning (half-day). Every meeting has an agenda and
owner. Cancel meetings that don't drive outcomes.

### Phase 4: Performance Management
Clear criteria for success. Regular feedback (not annual reviews). Address
underperformance early — the kindest thing is a fast, honest conversation.

## Output Format
Team management playbook with hiring scorecard, 30/60/90 onboarding plan,
operating cadence calendar, and performance review template.

## Common Pitfalls
1. **Hiring for competence only** — a skilled rep who damages team culture
   costs more than they produce.
2. **No documented playbook** — onboarding without a playbook is sink-or-swim.
3. **Avoiding performance conversations** — problems don't self-correct.
4. **Meetings without agendas** — status updates belong in async tools.
