# Randy Seidl — Relationship Selling for Enterprise AEs

**Sources:** Randy Seidl — [LinkedIn](https://www.linkedin.com/in/randyseidl/) · [Sales Community](https://www.salescommunity.com/) · [Tech Sales Insights podcast](https://tsi.salescommunity.com/) · [Your Go-To Sales Advisor](https://book.salescommunity.com/salesadvisor1) (with Tony Jeary) · [Building Trust, Expertise, and Outcomes](https://www.linkedin.com/pulse/building-trust-expertise-outcomes-randy-seidl-w1zje) · [Map Your Relationship ROI](https://www.linkedin.com/pulse/map-your-relationship-roi-randy-seidl-yxqde)

Randy Seidl is a former enterprise sales executive (EMC, StorageTek, Sun, HP $8B+ Americas) and founder of **Sales Community**, **Revenue Acceleration**, and **Top Talent Recruiting**. His public work centers on **relationship-led enterprise selling** — building durable trust across buyer organizations, not transactional outbound volume.

Use alongside **Marc Benioff** (trust-based enterprise CRM culture), **Andy Whyte** (MEDDICC evidence), and **Chris Walker** (demand creation / dark social awareness before the first meeting). Relationship selling fills the gap outbound sequences cannot: multi-year enterprise deals where the buyer chooses *you*, not just your product.

## When relationship selling wins vs outbound

| Situation | Lead with | Why |
|---|---|---|
| $100K+ ACV, 6+ month cycle, 5+ stakeholders | Relationship map + extended team | Sequences alone won't survive procurement or EB scrutiny |
| Warm intro, former customer, peer network | Relationship capital first | Outbound copy wastes existing trust |
| Expansion / renewal in installed base | Outcome history + stakeholder map | Champion-only deals churn when champion leaves |
| Net-new SMB/mid-market at scale | Outbound sequences (`cold-email-strategy`) | Relationship depth doesn't scale without automation |
| Buyer unknown, no signal | Outbound + social (`social-selling`) | Build awareness before asking for trust |

**Rule:** Outbound creates *access*. Relationship selling converts *access* into *trust* and *outcomes*. Don't skip outbound for cold accounts; don't rely on sequences alone for enterprise complexity.

## Three Plays in Every Sale

From *Your Go-To Sales Advisor* — every deal requires mastery of three dimensions simultaneously:

| Play | What it means | Failure mode |
|---|---|---|
| **1. Sell yourself** | Persistence, humility, integrity; listen more than talk; show up as advisor | Fake familiarity, over-talking, no relationship map |
| **2. Sell company + product** | Deep offering knowledge, elevator pitch, **extended internal team** (not lone wolf) | Product dump without stakeholder context |
| **3. Sell customer outcome** | Walk in their shoes; read their financials; tie deal to *their* success metrics | Feature selling; no quantified impact |

Long-term success requires all three — excelling at one while neglecting others caps career growth.

**Self-check questions (Seidl):**
- Are you a subject-matter expert on what you sell?
- Do you understand the results the customer is looking for?
- Have you built a **relationship map** inside the account?
- Do you know the **decision-making map** and your position in it?

## Relationship map (account-level)

Map people, not just titles. Seidl distinguishes day-to-day contacts, gatekeepers, influencers, and decision-makers.

### Map template

| Name | Role | Type | Trust (1–5) | Access | Last meaningful touch | Next action |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| | Economic Buyer | Decision | | | | |
| | Champion | Influencer | | | | |
| | Technical eval | User | | | | |
| | EA / gatekeeper | Enabler | | | | |
| | Procurement | Blocker? | | | | |
| | Internal SME ally | Partner | | | | |

**Types to include:**
- **Day-to-day** — operators who feel the pain daily
- **Gatekeepers** — EAs, chiefs of staff (relationship here unlocks access)
- **Decision map** — who signs, who blocks, who influences quietly
- **Extended team (your side)** — SE, CS, exec sponsor, product SME

### Multi-threading through relationships

Don't over-rely on one champion. Seidl's enterprise playbook:
1. Map minimum **3 buyer-side relationships** before stage 3 (evaluation)
2. Each thread serves a different play (self / product / outcome)
3. Bring **internal extended team** into customer conversations — lead them, don't solo
4. Attend internal training sessions to build SME network *before* you need them on a deal

Cross-ref: `multi-thread-orchestration` (orchestration), `pipeline-management` (MEDDICC Champion tests)

## Trust-building cadence (enterprise AE)

| Cadence | Activity | Seidl principle |
|---|---|---|
| Weekly | Update relationship map; one non-deal touch per key contact | "Always there for people" — authentic, not transactional |
| Per deal stage | Align internal extended team; brief SMEs on customer outcome | No lone wolf on large transactions |
| Monthly | Relationship ROI review — who have you helped without asking? | Relationships are assets, not accidents |
| Quarterly | In-person or on-site where possible — "go on sales calls, don't do just Zooms" | Behavior and presence outweigh slide decks |
| Ongoing | Read customer 10-K, investor deck, earnings — sell to *their* outcomes | Outcome play requires business literacy |

### Stakeholder trust scorecard

Score each mapped contact quarterly:

| Dimension | 1 (weak) | 3 (adequate) | 5 (strong) |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Responsiveness** | Ignores outreach | Replies within a week | Proactive check-ins |
| **Candor** | Surface-level only | Shares some blockers | Tells you real decision process |
| **Advocacy** | Neutral | Will take your meeting | Actively sells internally for you |
| **Outcome alignment** | Unclear fit | Interested in problem | Articulates mutual success criteria |
| **Relationship durability** | Tied to your logo | Knows you personally | Would take your call after you leave company |

**Red flag:** Average trust ≤ 2 on champion-only map → multi-thread immediately.

## Authentic relationship capital

Seidl's mirror test (WorkRamp / LEARN podcast recap):

> "People will leave companies and think they have relationships, but more times than not they have the relationship because of the business card, the company they work for, not really because of them."

**Build relationships that transfer:**
- Help customers, partners, and peers without immediate ask
- Take the call when they have a problem — even off-product
- Be authentic; "be a good person" is operational, not decorative
- Invest in peer community (Sales Community, Pavilion-style groups) for learning and network compound interest

**Anti-pattern — fake familiarity:** LinkedIn "great to connect!" + immediate pitch. Seidl's self-play requires *listening* and *presence*, not manufactured warmth.

## Behavior outweighs performance (coaching lens)

Managers should coach **behaviors** that build relationship capital:
- Did the rep update the relationship map this week?
- Did they bring an internal SME to a customer call?
- Did they read the customer's latest investor materials?
- Did they make a non-transactional touch (intro, article, intro to peer)?

**Coaching > inspecting pipeline fiction.** Pair with **Kevin Dorsey** cadence and **Jacco van der Kooij REKS** — relationship gaps often sit in **Skills** (stakeholder navigation) or **Knowledge** (customer business model), not **Efforts** (more emails).

## Relationship ROI map (personal network)

From [Map Your Relationship ROI](https://www.linkedin.com/pulse/map-your-relationship-roi-randy-seidl-yxqde) — treat network as portfolio:

| Tier | Who | Investment | Return expectation |
|---|---|---|---|
| **A — Inner circle** | Customers, mentors, exec sponsors | Monthly meaningful touch | Deal support, references, career |
| **B — Active network** | Peers, partners, analysts | Quarterly value exchange | Intel, intros, community |
| **C — Latent** | Conference contacts, dormant champions | Re-engage with value, not pitch | Warm paths when trigger fires |

Track like pipeline: last touch, value given, value received, next action.

## Anti-patterns

| Anti-pattern | Why it fails | Fix |
|---|---|---|
| **Champion-only deal** | Single point of failure; champion leaves = deal dies | Multi-thread per map above |
| **Fake familiarity** | Damages trust; reads as outbound in DMs | Listen-first; outcome-focused touches |
| **Lone wolf AE** | Large deals need SE, CS, exec, legal, product | Lead extended team early |
| **Logo relationships** | Don't survive job change | Personal authenticity + value delivery |
| **Product without outcome** | Wins short-term demos, loses EB | Read financials; quantify customer success |
| **Zoom-only enterprise** | Weak presence in complex sales | In-person/on-site when deal warrants |
