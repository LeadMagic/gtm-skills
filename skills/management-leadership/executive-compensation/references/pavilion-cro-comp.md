## Sam Jacobs / CRO Council Themes

1. **Revenue quality over bookings.** Variable comp increasingly multi-gated: ARR + NRR + efficiency.
2. **Efficient growth.** Rule of 40 and burn multiple influence CRO bonus more than in 2015–2020 era.
3. **Peer calibration.** CROs benchmark OTE, equity refresh, and severance against council cohort — reduces "random" board packages.
4. **Operator network for hire.** VP+ searches through Pavilion referrals outperform generic exec search for GTM fit.
5. **CRO School curriculum.** System-building: forecast discipline, board communication, org design — comp should reward these, not lone-wolf closes.

## Recommended CRO Gate Model (Pavilion-Aligned)

| Gate | Weight | Why |
|---|---:|---|
| Net new ARR | 40–50% | Growth accountability |
| NRR | 25–30% | Prevents junk revenue |
| Gross margin / Rule of 40 | 15–20% | Efficient scale |
| Team health (optional) | 5–10% | Attrition, ramp, diversity of pipeline |

## VP Sales (Pre-CRO) Pavilion Pattern

- Lower OTE than CRO; **higher weight on hiring and ramp milestones**
- Equity grant smaller but meaningful (0.25–1.0% FD)
- Severance 3–6 months — market for failed VP searches is expensive

## Using Pavilion in Recruiting

1. Post VP/CRO roles to Pavilion talent network (`job-posting-strategy`)
2. Ask candidates what **Pavilion cohort** or peer comps they reference
3. Board memo: "Package at [X] percentile of Pavilion/Bridge band for $[ARR] stage"
