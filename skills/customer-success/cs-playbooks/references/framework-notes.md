# Cs Playbooks — Framework Notes

Use these references to ground outputs in named, repeatable methodology.

## Primary Frameworks

- **Lincoln Murphy — Desired Outcome (DO) Methodology.** Customer success is not about "making customers happy" — it's about driving the customer toward their Desired Outcome. Every playbook action must be traceable to a customer DO. Murphy's framework distinguishes between appropriate experience (the level of CS investment per account based on ACV) and inappropriate over-service that destroys unit economics.

- **Gainsight — Customer Health Score Framework.** Gainsight operationalizes CS playbooks through health scoring: product usage (adoption depth, feature breadth, login frequency), engagement (QBR attendance, support ticket sentiment, NPS/CSAT trends), and commercial health (NRR, expansion pipeline, renewal risk). The health score triggers playbook actions — a score drop should fire a play.

- **Winning by Design — SPICED Post-Sale Framework.** WbD's SPICED adapts to the post-sale context: Situation (current deployment state), Pain (adoption friction, unrealized value), Impact (cost of churn or under-adoption), Critical Event (renewal date, champion departure, competitor PoC), Decision (expansion, renewal, or advocacy). Playbooks should trigger on Critical Events, not calendar dates.

## Operating Assumptions

- Playbooks are trigger-based, not calendar-based: a health score drop or champion departure fires the playbook.
- One-size CSM ratios are a trap: appropriate experience by ACV tier determines playbook depth per segment.
- The escalation playbook is the most important: what happens when the customer is at risk determines retention.
