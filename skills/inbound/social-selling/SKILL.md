# Social Selling

## Overview

LinkedIn isn't a broadcasting platform — it's a research and relationship
tool. The best social sellers use it to understand prospects before reaching
out, build context that makes outreach feel personal, and create a professional
presence that prospects check before responding.

This skill covers LinkedIn Sales Navigator workflows, DM sequencing, profile
optimization for trust-building, and the Social Selling Index (SSI) as a
measurement framework.

## Authoritative Foundations

LinkedIn's own Social Selling Index (SSI) measures four dimensions: professional
brand, finding the right people, engaging with insights, and building relationships.
LinkedIn data shows that SSI leaders create 45% more opportunities and are 51%
more likely to hit quota.

## Prerequisites

- LinkedIn profile (personal, not company page)
- Sales Navigator subscription (strongly recommended for B2B outbound)
- Content strategy (at minimum: what topics you post about)

## Step-by-Step Process

### Phase 1: Profile Optimization

Your profile is your prospect's first stop after receiving your outreach.
It must build trust in under 10 seconds:

- **Headline**: Not your job title. Your value prop. "Helping [ICP] solve [problem]"
- **About**: Problem → Solution → Proof → CTA. Write in first person.
- **Featured**: Pin your best case study, article, or free tool
- **Activity**: 2-3 posts/week minimum. Prospects check this before responding
- **Recommendations**: 5+ from clients or colleagues. Social proof wins

### Phase 2: Sales Navigator Workflows

For filter stacks, saved-search alerts, and filter-specific message libraries,
load `sales-navigator-prospecting` (Morgan J. Ingram / AMP). Minimum high-intent
filters: Posted last 30 days, Following your company, Viewed your profile, tenure
< 1 year and 6–12 months.

1. **Lead search**: Filter by title, company size, industry, geography, and
   recent activity (posted in last 30 days = active user = more likely to respond)

2. **Account research**: Before reaching out to anyone, check their LinkedIn
   activity. What did they post about? What did they comment on? What articles
   did they share? This is your personalization material.

3. **Saved leads**: Create lead lists by segment. Monitor for job changes,
   new posts, and company updates.

### Phase 3: DM Sequencing

1. **Connection request** (no pitch): "Saw your post on [topic] — really
   resonated with the point about [specific thing]. Would love to connect."
2. **After acceptance** — wait 2-3 days
3. **DM 1** — Value drop: share an insight, article, or observation. No ask.
4. **DM 2** — Soft CTA: "We help [similar companies] with [problem]. Worth a
   quick exploration?"
5. **Voice note** (optional, high-intent only): 30-60 second personal message

Rules: No pitching in the first message. No links in connection requests.
No "I'd love to tell you about my company." Give before you ask.

### Phase 4: Content-to-Conversation

Posting isn't about going viral. It's about creating conversation starters
with your ICP. For reach mechanics — format rankings, dwell time, link
penalties, golden hour — load `linkedin-algorithm` (Richard van der Blom's
Algorithm Insights Report). For weekly **LinkedIn Live** as the content engine
(WinsDay / Reveting model) — load `linkedin-live-strategy` (Jessie Lizak).
Post about:
- Problems your ICP faces (not your product)
- Data and insights from your work
- Customer stories (anonymized, with permission)
- Industry observations that show you understand their world

When someone engages with your post, that's a warm opening for a DM.

## Common Pitfalls

1. **Pitching in the first DM.** LinkedIn is a relationship platform. Pitching
   before giving value gets you ignored or blocked.

2. **Empty profile.** A prospect receives your cold email, checks your LinkedIn,
   and finds 12 connections and a blank about section. Trust destroyed.

3. **Posting company content only.** Prospects follow people, not companies.
   Post your perspective, your insights, your experience.

4. **No Sales Navigator.** Free LinkedIn is a research tool. Sales Navigator
   is a prospecting engine. The $99/month pays for itself in one meeting.

5. **Inconsistent activity.** Posting once a month looks like you're not
   serious. 2-3 posts/week minimum to maintain visibility.
