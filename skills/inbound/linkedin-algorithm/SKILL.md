# LinkedIn Algorithm Optimization

## Overview

Most LinkedIn underperformance is not a content-quality problem — it's a
mechanics problem. Creators post the right message in the wrong format, kill
reach with external links and hashtag habits from 2021, and misread a
platform-wide reach compression as a personal failure.

This skill applies Richard van der Blom's Algorithm Insights Report — the
largest recurring public study of LinkedIn organic performance (the 2026
edition analyzed 1.3M+ posts and 80,000+ profiles) — to diagnose reach
problems and design posts that work with the algorithm instead of against it.

## Prerequisites

- Personal LinkedIn profile (and company page if optimizing page reach)
- 90 days of post analytics (impressions, engagement, followers) as baseline
- Defined content pillars (see `founder-brand` / `social-media-strategy`)

## Step-by-Step Process

### Phase 1: Diagnose Before Changing Anything

1. Pull the last 90 days of posts into a sheet: format, length, links,
   hashtags, tags, posting time, impressions, reactions, comments, saves.
2. Separate platform-wide compression from personal decline. Van der Blom's
   data showed broad YoY declines (views -50%, engagement -25%, follower
   growth -59% in the 2025 edition) — benchmark against the trend, not 2023.
3. Flag reach killers: external links in body, 5+ hashtags, 5+ person tags,
   posting more than once per day, AI-sounding text.

### Phase 2: Fix Format Mix

Rank formats by the report's reach/engagement multipliers (full table in
`references/richard-van-der-blom-algorithm.md`):

- **Document posts (PDF carousels)** — top format: ~1.39x reach, highest
  engagement rate (~6.6%). Use for frameworks, benchmarks, tutorials.
- **Short native video (30-90s)** — strong engagement (~5.6%) but average
  reach dropped below baseline (~0.86x); use for thought leadership, not reach.
- **Image + text** — the volume workhorse; solid middle performance.
- **Text-only** — works when sharp and short; weakest default format.
- **External links in body** — significant reach penalty (roughly 25-60%
  depending on profile type). Put links in comments or on your profile.

### Phase 3: Engineer Post Anatomy

The report's four-phase post system: **writing → publishing → nurturing →
repurposing**.

- **Hook:** first 2-3 lines decide dwell time. Numbers, credibility, or
  tension before the fold.
- **Length:** sharper beats longer in 2026 — 34% of top-performing posts are
  now under 600 characters (up from 21% in 2024). Dwell time is weighted by
  completion rate, so cut ruthlessly.
- **Keywords over hashtags:** posts without hashtags now outperform posts
  with them by 5-10%. Write topical keywords into the body instead.
- **Tags:** tag 5+ people and the algorithm treats it as a spam signal if
  most don't engage. Tag only people who will respond.

### Phase 4: Work the Engagement Window

1. **Golden hour:** reply to every comment in the first 60-90 minutes —
   comment threads (depth, quality, length) are weighted above reactions.
2. **Saves are a ranking signal:** design "save-worthy" reference content
   (checklists, benchmarks, frameworks).
3. **Pre/post activity:** comment meaningfully on ICP-relevant posts before
   and after publishing; engagement is reciprocal in the interest graph.
4. **Frequency cap:** 3-5 posts/week, never more than 1/day.

### Phase 5: Company Page and Employee Advocacy

Company page reach grew ~33% in the 2026 data while personal profile reach
fell — pages are no longer dead. Run pillar-based page content, engage from
the page identity, and pair with employee advocacy (employees resharing with
their own commentary, not bare reposts). Amplify proven organic winners with
thought leadership ads (see `paid-social-strategy`).

## Output Format

LinkedIn algorithm audit + optimization plan: 90-day baseline table, reach-killer
findings, format mix recommendation with multipliers, post anatomy checklist
(hook/length/keywords/tags), golden-hour engagement routine, posting calendar
(frequency + timing), and a 30-day remeasurement plan.

## Quality Check

- [ ] Baseline separates platform-wide compression from personal decline
- [ ] Every recommendation cites a report finding, not folklore
- [ ] Format mix includes document posts for save-worthy content
- [ ] No external links in post bodies; links moved to comments/profile
- [ ] Hashtags removed or capped; keywords written into body copy
- [ ] Golden-hour engagement routine scheduled, not aspirational
- [ ] 30-day remeasurement date set with the same metrics

## Common Pitfalls

1. **Optimizing for followers.** The interest graph killed follower-count
   reach. Topic authority — consistent pillars, keyword repetition — is the
   new distribution asset.

2. **Posting links in the body.** The single most common self-inflicted reach
   penalty. Move links to the first comment or let your profile link convert
   (Adam Robinson's zero-click pattern).

3. **Long-form by default.** Dwell time is now combined with completion rate.
   A 2,000-character post most readers abandon scores worse than a sharp
   500-character post they finish.

4. **Publishing AI-sounding content.** AI-generated text is measurably
   penalized in both reach and engagement. Use AI for research and editing,
   not voice.

5. **Treating engagement as optional.** Ignoring comments in the first hour
   wastes the algorithm's evaluation window. Nurturing is part of the post,
   not an afterthought.

6. **Chasing every algorithm rumor.** The report debunks 20 LinkedIn myths
   per edition. If a tactic isn't backed by data, treat it as folklore.
