# Ad Creative Strategy

## Overview

Creative is the single biggest lever in paid advertising performance. A great
creative on a mediocre audience outperforms a mediocre creative on a perfect
audience. This skill covers creative formats per platform, testing methodology,
fatigue management, and scaling creative production.

## When to Use

- "Create ad creative for LinkedIn"
- "Develop a creative testing plan"
- "Our ads are fatigued — refresh creative"
- "What creative formats work on each platform?"
- "Scale our ad creative production"
- "AI-generated ad creative"

## Step-by-Step Process

### Phase 1: Creative Formats by Platform

**LinkedIn Ads:**
- Single image: 1200x627px. 1-2 lines of text overlay. Best for: offers, events.
- Carousel: 1080x1080px per card, 3-10 cards. Best for: storytelling, data.
- Video: 3-30 seconds. Square (1:1) or vertical (4:5). Best for: product demos.
- Document ads: PDF uploaded as carousel. Best for: guides, reports, templates.

**Google Ads:**
- Responsive search ads: 15 headlines (30 chars), 4 descriptions (90 chars).
  Google mixes and optimizes. Feed all 15 — more assets = better performance.
- Display: 1200x628, 300x250, 728x90, 160x600. Static or HTML5.
- YouTube: 15-30 seconds skippable. Hook in first 5 seconds.

**Meta (FB/IG):**
- Feed: 1080x1080 (square) or 1080x1350 (vertical). Video or static.
- Stories: 1080x1920 (full vertical). 15 seconds max.
- Lead forms: same creative specs, plus form questions.

**TikTok:**
- 9:16 vertical video. 15-60 seconds. Native-looking (not polished corporate).
- Hook in first 2 seconds. Text overlay. Captions. Trending audio optional.

### Phase 2: Creative Testing Methodology

**Always test:** At minimum 2-3 variants running simultaneously. Never one
creative at a time.

Test one variable at a time:
- Headline/hook (most impact)
- Visual style (photo vs illustration vs screenshot)
- Offer/CTA (demo vs trial vs guide)
- Social proof placement
- Format (single image vs carousel vs video)

**Testing cadence:**
- Launch 3-5 variants → run 7-14 days → kill underperformers → launch new
  variants against winners → repeat.

**Platform minimums for statistical significance:**
- LinkedIn: 1,000+ impressions per variant
- Google: 100+ clicks per variant
- Meta: 50+ conversions per variant per week
- TikTok: 5,000+ impressions per variant

### Phase 3: Creative Fatigue Management

Creative fatigue = performance drops because audience has seen the ad too
many times. Signs: CTR declining, CPC rising, frequency >3-4.

**Prevention:**
- Rotate creative every 2-3 weeks.
- Maintain 3-5 active variants per ad set.
- Use platform automated creative refresh (Meta Advantage+ Creative).
- Segment audiences to reduce overlap.

### Phase 4: Scaling Creative Production

**Traditional model:** agency → brief → concept → production → 2-4 assets/month.
$3-10K/month. Slow but polished.

**AI-augmented model:** 1 in-house creative + AI tools → 10-20 assets/week.
$1-3K/month (tools + person). Fast, testable volume beats polished scarcity.

**AI creative tools:**
- Ad copy: ChatGPT, Claude, Jasper (write multiple headline/body variants).
- Image generation: Midjourney, DALL-E (concept visuals, backgrounds).
- Video: Arcads, Creatify (AI-generated UGC-style ads from scripts).
- Iteration: Canva AI, Adobe Firefly (resize, remix, translate).

**The volume advantage:** A $5K/month agency producing 4 polished assets/month
loses to a $2K/month AI-augmented workflow producing 40 testable variants/month.
Testing velocity beats production quality.

Creative strategy document with: platform-specific format guide, testing
plan (what to test first), fatigue management rules, creative production
workflow, and creative asset tracker.

## Common Pitfalls

1. **One creative at a time.** "Let's see how this performs" with no comparison.
   You can't optimize what you don't test.

2. **Polished over volume.** A beautiful ad nobody sees loses to 10 rough
   variants that find the winning message.

3. **No fatigue monitoring.** Same ad for 8 weeks with frequency >10. CTR
   drops 50%+. Rotate before performance degrades.

4. **Creative not matching platform context.** A polished corporate video on
   TikTok looks like an ad. Native-looking content wins.

5. **Testing too many variables at once.** Changed headline AND visual AND CTA.
   No idea which change caused the lift. One variable per test.
