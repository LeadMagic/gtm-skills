# Content Distribution — Framework Notes

Reference index for `SKILL.md`. Apply named frameworks to justify recommendations.

## Primary Frameworks

- **Content Distribution Matrix**
- **Gary Vaynerchuk Content Model**
- **Ann Handley — Everybody Writes**

## Authoritative foundations

- **Content Distribution Matrix** — Creating content is 20% of the work; distribution is 80%. Map every piece of content to specific channels, formats, and audiences. A blog post without a distribution plan is an archive entry, not a growth asset.
- **Gary Vaynerchuk Content Model** — One piece of pillar content spawns dozens of micro-content derivatives per platform. Each platform gets a native-format version: LinkedIn carousel, Twitter thread, TikTok talking-head, Instagram infographic, newsletter excerpt. AI handles the reformatting; the human maintains the core message across formats.
- **Ann Handley — Everybody Writes** — Distribution quality depends on writing quality. A well-distributed piece of weak content still doesn't convert. Handley's principle: write for one specific person, use concrete language, and edit ruthlessly before amplifying.

## Process phases

- **Phase 1: Content Audit** — Inventory existing content, identify top performers by engagement and conversion
- **Phase 2: Atomization** — Break each pillar piece into 10+ derivative formats per platform (LinkedIn posts, Twitter threads, video clips, infographics, email excerpts, podcast snippets)
- **Phase 3: Channel Matrix** — Assign each derivative to the optimal channel with format specs, posting time, and frequency
- **Phase 4: Distribution Calendar** — Build a 30-day publishing calendar with paid amplification budget for top performers
