## Primary Frameworks

- **Cialdini Visual Persuasion**
- **Tufte Data Visualization**
- **Ann Handley — Everybody Writes**

## Authoritative foundations

- **Cialdini Visual Persuasion** — Robert Cialdini's principles (reciprocity, scarcity, authority, consistency, liking, social proof, unity) applied to visual design. Social proof in GTM: customer logos, testimonial photos, review ratings, case study metrics in hero sections. Scarcity: limited-time offer badges, countdown timers, 'only X spots left.'
- **Tufte Data Visualization** — Edward Tufte's principles for truthful, dense data display. Maximize data-ink ratio. Remove chartjunk (3D effects, unnecessary gridlines, decorative elements). In GTM: every chart in a pitch deck or one-pager should survive the 'Tufte test' — can the viewer extract the insight in under 5 seconds?
- **Ann Handley — Everybody Writes** — Writing quality in visual context. A beautifully designed graphic with weak copy fails. Handley's rules for GTM visuals: write for one person, use specific language (numbers > adjectives), and every visual element must serve the communication goal.
