## Authoritative foundations

- **Andrej Karpathy — 'Vibe coding' applied to marketing** — Describe what you want, let the AI generate, then iterate fast. The skill isn't prompt engineering — it's taste, direction, and knowing what to keep vs redo. Applied to marketing: describe a campaign, get 10 variants, pick the best, refine.
- **Gary Vaynerchuk — Content at scale: 64 pieces per platform per day** — Volume is reach. AI makes post-2024 volume possible: one long-form piece spawns 64 micro-content derivatives per platform. The math: more content = more impressions = more conversion events.
- **Dave Gerhardt (Exit Five) — B2B marketing that doesn't feel like marketing** — The bar in B2B is low. Most marketing reads like a press release. AI makes it easy to sound corporate. Gerhardt's principle: write like a human who knows the buyer's problem, not like a company describing its features.
- **Amanda Natividad (SparkToro) — Zero-click content, audience-first AI** — Deliver full value in the feed without requiring a click. AI can generate 10x more content — but the strategic question is what to publish where to build audience trust before asking for conversion.
- **Harry Dry (Marketing Examples) — Show, don't tell (visual proof)** — Specific numbers and screenshots beat adjectives. AI can write claims; the human must verify and attach proof. Every marketing claim needs a source, a screenshot, or a customer quote.
- **Justin Welsh — Content operating system, repurposing lattice** — A single blog post becomes 5 LinkedIn posts, 2 Twitter threads, 1 newsletter, 1 podcast clip, and 1 YouTube short. AI handles the reformatting; the human maintains the signal across formats.
