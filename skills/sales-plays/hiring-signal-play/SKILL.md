# Hiring Signal Outbound Play

## Overview
A company posting to hire a VP Sales, CRO, RevOps lead, or SDR manager signals
one thing: they're investing in revenue. This is a buying moment. The new hire
will evaluate and change tools within their first 90 days. This play targets
both the hiring manager (who just got budget) and the new hire (who will build
their stack).

## Core Principle
> Job postings are the most overlooked buying signal in B2B. A company doesn't
> hire a VP Sales to maintain the status quo — they hire to build, change, and
> scale. Your window: the 90 days after the hire starts, when they're
> evaluating everything.

## Step-by-Step Process

### Phase 1: Signal Detection
Monitor job boards and LinkedIn for:
- **Hiring Manager:** VP Sales, CRO, Head of Revenue, VP Growth (decision-maker with budget)
- **Builder:** RevOps Manager, Head of Sales Ops, Sales Enablement Manager (builds the stack)
- **Scaler:** SDR Manager, Head of Outbound (increases team size, needs tools)
- **Specialist:** Head of Partnerships, Customer Success Director (expands into new motions)

### Phase 2: Timing Strategy
- **Week 1-2 of posting:** Reach out to CEO/Founder (they just approved the budget)
- **Week 3-8:** The role likely gets filled. Research who they hired.
- **Week 1-30 of new hire:** Outreach to the new hire. They're in "evaluation mode."
- **Week 30-60:** New hire is building their team and tech stack.
- **Week 60-90:** New hire is executing. If you're not in the stack by now,
  you've missed the window.

### Phase 3: CEO/Founder Outreach (Week 1-2 of posting)
Subject: "Saw the VP Sales role — question"
Body: "Saw you're hiring a VP Sales. Most teams at your stage I work with
are dealing with [X problem] that new VP will have to solve. How are you
thinking about this as you build out the GTM function?"
Angle: You're not selling yet — you're offering perspective from working
with similar teams. Peer-to-peer, insight-first.

### Phase 4: New Hire Outreach (Week 1-30 post-hire)
Subject: "Assuming you're evaluating [their domain]"
Body: Reference their previous company (what you know about their stack there),
connect to what they likely need to build here. "Most people coming from [X]
into [Y] find [Z problem]. How are you thinking about this?"
Angle: You understand their world because you've seen this transition before.

### Phase 5: Multi-Thread Touch Plan
- Week 1: Email to CEO/Founder
- Week 3: LinkedIn connection to new hire (if announced)
- Week 5: Email to new hire (first outreach)
- Week 8: LinkedIn DM to new hire with relevant content
- Week 12: Breakup — "Assuming you've got this covered." (often gets reply)
