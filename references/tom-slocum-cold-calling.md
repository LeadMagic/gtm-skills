## Core Thesis

SDR teams stall on **clarity**, not effort. Cold calling works when reps sell **the meeting, not the product** — nurse, not doctor. Research → relatability → relevancy (3Rs) before every block; quality conversations beat dial volume.

> "Your job isn't to close the deal on the first call. Your job is to sell the meeting." — Slocum

---

## Sell the Meeting (Nurse, Not Doctor)

| Role | Job on cold call |
|---|---|
| **SDR (nurse)** | Surface pain, tease solution, book qualified meeting |
| **AE (doctor)** | Diagnose deeply, prescribe, close |

On the call:
1. What's the problem they're solving?
2. What bottlenecks block day-to-day goals?
3. What's the big goal?
4. Tease how you might help — **do not full-pitch**
5. Close to 20–30 minute meeting with clear agenda

## 3Rs Framework — Research, Relatability, Relevancy

Foundation of SD Lab outbound coaching (also called **3×3 method**):

**3×3 rule:** Find **3 insights in under 3 minutes** before dialing or emailing.

| Insight slot | What to capture |
|---|---|
| 1 | Why this **account** (signal, trigger, fit) |
| 2 | Why this **person** (role, post, career move) |
| 3 | Why **now** (timing hook for opener) |

**Where to look:** LinkedIn profile → company page → peer/community context

Store all three in CRM so every touch has context — **real signal, not fake personalization**.

Slocum now advocates AI-assisted research workflows to hit 3×3 speed at scale while keeping relevancy.

## Dial Discipline & Call Blocks

**Backend prep (outside dial hours):**
- Refine data, tier accounts, prioritize prospects
- Complete 3×3 research per contact
- One clear reason + one relevant observation + one clean question per touch

**During call block:**
- Focus until the "buzzer" stops — quality over arbitrary dial count
- Consultative tone; confidence from prep, not scripts
- Fail fast: more reps + more dials = fewer curveballs (with prep)

**Channel coordination:**
- Track what gets email replies vs phone connects
- Layer touches on same accounts — channels reinforce, not compete

## Call Block Setup Checklist

- [ ] 3×3 research complete for block's contact list
- [ ] Tier/priority order set (align with Reisert Buckets or Gilkey Phone Intent)
- [ ] Opener = one account signal + permission (not product pitch)
- [ ] CRM fields populated before first dial
- [ ] Meeting goal defined (agenda teaser ready)
- [ ] Manager/coach slot for intro-call review (Slocum reviews SDR intros weekly)
- [ ] Email/LI touches scheduled around same accounts

## When Phone Beats Email

| Phone (Slocum) | Email/LI |
|---|---|
| 3×3 research creates warm opener | Initial touch before call (warm calling) |
| Complex pain needs live probing | Simple value prop + case study |
| High-intent tier from prep | Nurture after Not Now disposition |
| SDR skill development via live reps | Async enterprise committees |

Slocum: top of funnel is the bottleneck **right now** — getting conversations and right targets beats follow-up optimization alone.

---

## Anti-Patterns

1. **Closing on the cold call** — SDR overreach; kills trust
2. **Robotic templated pitches** — no relatability
3. **Dialing without 3×3 prep** — generic = ignored
4. **Volume without tiering** — heroics, not systems
5. **Channels siloed** — email and phone unaware of each other
