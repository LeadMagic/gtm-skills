## Core Model — Teach, Tailor, Take Control

Challenger sellers **teach** buyers something new about their business, **tailor** the message to stakeholder priorities, and **take control** of the sale (commercial tension without aggression).

| Pillar | Rep behavior | GTM artifact |
|---|---|---|
| **Teach** | Lead with insight that reframes the problem | Insight deck, "cost of inaction" math |
| **Tailor** | Map message to economic vs technical vs user buyers | Stakeholder-specific talk tracks |
| **Take control** | Push on timeline, mutual action plan, next steps | MAP template in CRM |

**Rep profiles (research):** Challengers outperform in complex B2B; Relationship Builders underperform in high-consideration deals. Train insight-led teaching, not rapport-only discovery.

## Commercial Teaching Insight

Build **one insight** per ICP segment:

1. **Warmer** — Credible observation about their world ("Most [ICP] teams we see…")
2. **Reframe** — "The real issue isn't X — it's Y"
3. **Rational drowning** — Quantify cost of status quo (metrics buyer accepts)
4. **Emotional impact** — What happens to them if nothing changes
5. **A new way** — Category approach (not product pitch yet)
6. **Your solution** — Bridge only after reframe lands

Use in **first meeting** and **objection reframing** — not as a product demo opener.

---

## When to Use

- Complex B2B with multiple stakeholders and status-quo bias
- "We're fine with our current vendor" / status-quo objections
- Enabling AEs on **value messaging** beyond feature lists
- Pair with **Keenan Gap Selling** (diagnosis) + **Challenger** (reframe)

## When NOT to Use

- Transactional SKU sales with no teaching window
- As substitute for **MEDDICC** evidence on enterprise deals
- Replacing **JOLT** on late-stage indecision — Challenger opens; JOLT closes stall
