# Email Copywriting Frameworks

Shared reference for outbound and copywriting skills.

## Core Frameworks

### AIDA
Attention → Interest → Desire → Action. The oldest and most universal framework.

### PAS
Problem → Agitation → Solution. Works well for pain-point-driven outreach.

### BAB
Before → After → Bridge. "Here's where you are. Here's where you could be. Here's how to get there."

### SPARK
Signal → Problem → Answer → Result → Key takeaway. ColdIQ's framework for trigger-based outreach.

### Observation → Relevance → CTA
The 3-line cold email framework. Highest-performing structure in 2026. Under 80 words.

## Subject Line Principles

- 2-4 words, lowercase, no punctuation tricks
- Should look like it came from a colleague ("reply rates," "hiring ops," "Q2 forecast")
- No product pitches, no urgency, no emojis
- No prospect's first name — it signals automation

## Voice Guidelines

- Sound like a sharp, thoughtful human — not a sales machine
- Use contractions. Read it aloud. If it sounds like marketing copy, rewrite it.
- "You/your" should dominate over "I/we"
- Don't open with who you are or what your company does

## What To Avoid

- "I hope this email finds you well" or "My name is X and I work at Y"
- "Leverage," "synergy," "circle back," "best-in-class," "leading provider"
- Feature dumps — one proof point beats ten features
- HTML, images, or multiple links
- "Just checking in" follow-ups
- Asking for 30-minute calls in first touch
- Fake "Re:" or "Fwd:" subject lines

## Personalization Tiers

### Tier 1 — Merge Fields
`{first_name}`, `{company}`, `{title}` swapped in. Generic template. 1-2% reply rate.

### Tier 2 — Segment-Specific
Different templates per role, industry, or signal type. Swapped pain points and proof points. 3-5% reply rate.

### Tier 3 — Deep Per-Lead
Unique opening per lead. Signal-anchored first line that references something specific. 8-12% reply rate. Cap at 50-100 leads. Use AI sub-agents.

## Sequence Structure

Total: 4-7 touches over 14-25 days (3-day gaps optimal).

- Touch 1: Cold intro — Signal + Proof + Low-friction CTA (50-90 words)
- Touch 2: New angle — Different value prop or use case (30-50 words)
- Touch 3: Social proof — Case study, metric, customer quote (30-50 words)
- Touch 4: Resource — Article, guide, calculator relevant to their problem
- Touch 5: Objection pre-handle — Address common concerns before they're raised
- Touch 6: Re-frame — Different lens on the problem
- Touch 7: Breakup — Final touch. Honor it. (20-40 words)
