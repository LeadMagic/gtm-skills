**Naming collision:** Two "reverse demo" traditions exist in repo:

## Great Demo Structure

| Phase | Goal | Time share |
|---|---|---|
| **Discovery / Doing Discovery** | Understand before showing | 30–50% of cycle |
| **Demo flow** | Show **their** scenarios, not feature tour | Single narrative arc |
| **Do Something / Do Again** | Hands-on moments that prove value | 2–3 "wow" beats max |
| **Next steps** | Mutual close plan | Before leaving call |

**Rule:** If you demo before discovery completes, you train the buyer to treat you as a vendor — not a partner.

---

## Cohan Reverse Demo (Discovery Variant)

When displacing an incumbent:

1. Ask buyer to **screen-share their current workflow** (incumbent tool)
2. Map pain live — "show me where this breaks"
3. Only then demo **the gap moments** — not full product
4. Document their words as before/after proof

**Contrast Varun Anand reverse demo:** Clay-style = prospect explores *your* product with guidance. Cohan-style = prospect exposes *incumbent* pain first.

---

## Demo Mistakes (Cohan)

| Mistake | Fix |
|---|---|
| Feature tour ("let me show you everything") | Scenario-led: 3 problems → 3 proofs |
| Happy ears after demo | Confirm impact metrics before next meeting |
| Multiple presenters talking over each other | Single demo driver + note-taker |
| Skipping "Do Something" | Buyer must click/type once |
