## Core Frameworks (Aneesh Lal / Wishly)

### 1. Awareness → Education → Conversion

Wishly's three-step campaign arc for B2B brands (PartnerStack partner profile):

| Stage | Goal | Typical formats |
|---|---|---|
| **Awareness** | Shift mindset; reach ICP on LinkedIn | Sponsored posts, boosted creator video, carousel hooks |
| **Education** | Build trust before in-market | Co-authored guides, newsletters, podcast segments, fireside chats |
| **Conversion** | Capture intent | Comment CTAs, dedicated landing pages, creator-led customer calls |

**Rule:** Do not buy "a couple of posts" alone — bundle the creator's full audience surface (95% of Wishly creators have newsletter, podcast, or secondary channel per Aneesh).

### 2. ICP-Aligned Creator Selection (Not Vanity Followers)

| Signal | Weight | Anti-pattern |
|---|---|---|
| Audience job titles match ICP | High | 300K generic Instagram followers |
| Engagement from target personas | High | Raw like count without profile scrape |
| Creator has used/demoed product | Required | Billboard-style endorsement |
| Newsletter/podcast ICP overlap | High | LinkedIn-only one-off post |

**Operational hack (public):** Scrape public likers/commenters into Clay; score against ABM list — overlap % becomes CFO conversation fuel (G2 interview).

### 3. Multi-Channel Creator Bundle

Standard Wishly campaign stack:

1. LinkedIn posts (organic + optional boost)
2. Newsletter placement
3. Podcast mention or guest spot
4. Co-authored ebook / guide / checklist
5. Fireside chat or webinar
6. Optional: creator joins brand customer calls

**Renewal signal:** ~90% pilot → long-term renewal when bundles run 3+ months with bi-weekly reviews (Netinfluencer, G2).

### 4. Problem-First Hooks (Software)

For SaaS, Aneesh advocates **problem and blind-spot framing** over feature lists:

- Lead with cost of *not* solving (wasted time, missed pipeline)
- Avoid "saves X hours" claims every competitor makes
- Use emotional urgency for buyers who do not know the category yet

Cross-link: `cold-email-strategy` (problem-first outbound) · `positioning-messaging`.

### 5. Authenticity & Quality Control

Non-negotiables from public interviews:

- Creators must genuinely use or thoroughly demo before endorsing
- Agency mediates expectations — avoid transactional marketplace matching
- Vet reliability (creators who ghost damage the category for everyone)
- Dedicated campaign strategist aligns brand objectives with creator narrative

### 6. Operational Cadence (Brand Side)

| Milestone | Action |
|---|---|
| **Pre-launch** | Upfront vetting, creative brief, ICP overlap analysis |
| **Week 0–2** | First deliverables live; engagement scrape baseline |
| **Bi-weekly** | Performance review, audience quality, next deliverables |
| **Day 90** | Renewal decision — pipeline signals + landing page traffic vs benchmark |
| **Post-campaign** | Continue engaging leads from comments/DMs — do not stop at impressions |

### 7. CFO Pitch Stack

Three steps Aneesh cites for finance skeptics:

1. **Identify finance metrics** — what does the CFO need to green-light? (pipeline influenced, account overlap, CAC comparison)
2. **Case studies** — comparable B2B campaigns with documented outcomes (e.g., $200K spend → 7:1 return in 4 months cited in PartnerStack profile)
3. **Data specificity** — ABM list ∩ creator audience overlap via Sales Navigator + Clay

## B2B vs B2C Influencer (Aneesh's Public Position)

| Dimension | B2B (LinkedIn) | B2C (IG/TikTok) |
|---|---|---|
| Buyer readiness | Embraced by tech/SaaS; industry-specific creators | More skepticism; saturation & bot risk |
| Audience value | High-income professionals ("keeners") | Discretionary spend varies |
| Trust model | Long-tenure SME advice | Entertainment + aspiration |
| Content | Short video, carousels, co-authored depth | Repurposed short-form |
| Attribution | Landing pages + CRM lookback + dark social | Last-click heavier |

## Employee Advocacy vs External Creators

| | Employee advocates | External B2B creators |
|---|---|---|
| **Trust** | Insider credibility, product depth | Third-party validation, category authority |
| **Reach** | Limited to employee networks | Aggregated ICP audiences at scale |
| **Cost** | Enablement + governance | Agency fees + creator compensation |
| **Risk** | Off-brand posts, compliance | Authenticity failures, wrong ICP fit |
| **Best for** | Product launches, hiring brand, customer proof | Category education, pipeline acceleration |

**Combine:** External creators for **awareness**; employee advocates for **conversion proof** and **customer marketing** (`customer-marketing` advocacy ladder).

## Anti-Patterns (From Public Content)

1. **Vanity followers** — optimizing for count vs ICP relevance
2. **Single-post campaigns** — no newsletter/podcast/landing page follow-through
3. **No attribution plan** — expecting last-click UTMs on dark social
4. **Wrong ICP creators** — sales creator for HR tech without audience overlap check
5. **Transactional marketplace** — no strategist, no bi-weekly review
6. **Solution-first software hooks** — feature lists that blend with competitors
7. **Stopping at impressions** — no post-engagement lead nurture
