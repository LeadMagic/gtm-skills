## Core Thesis

Outbound is not dead — unstructured activity is. Reps should always do the **highest-value task available**, working **backwards** from booked meetings to net-new targets. Track **completions** (full conversations), not vanity dials.

> "Within 3 clicks, you're in your next best conversation." — Outbound Operators positioning (shared with Ronen Pessar)

## The Buckets Framework — Four CRM Stages

Mirror these as contact-level CRM stages (labels flexible; logic is not):

| Bucket | Stage | Rep action | Volume target |
|---|---|---|---|
| **4 — Scheduled** | Meeting booked | Confirm week-of, 24h before, 2–3h before | Protect show rate |
| **3 — Priority (Working)** | Engaged — answered, replied, referred | Call 2–3×/day; every touch in CRM | Hot leads first after Bucket 4 |
| **2 — In Cadence** | ICP contacted, no engagement yet | Execute multi-touch cadence | ~100 contacts always in bucket |
| **1 — Uncontacted (Target)** | New ICP, researched, not yet dialed | Research + qualify before first dial | Only ICP-fit enters |

### Daily workflow (backwards)

1. Confirm all Bucket 4 meetings
2. Work Bucket 3 hot leads
3. Execute Bucket 2 cadence touches
4. Add researched contacts to Bucket 1 → first dial

**Math of sales:** with phone-intent prioritization ("picker uppers"), teams can move from ~5 conversations per 100 dials to ~25 — same effort, 5× conversations.

## Call Structure (No Fluff)

**Opener — pattern interrupt + permission:**
> "Hi [name], this is [name] from [company]. I know you weren't expecting my call — can I have 30 seconds to tell you why I'm calling, and you tell me if it's worth continuing?"

**Never:** "Did I catch you at a bad time?" / "How are you today?"

**Reason:** account-specific trigger (funding, hiring, tech change)

**Discovery:** 11–14 questions; SPIN structure; talk less than you listen

**Close to next step:** specific agenda + concrete time — "Does Thursday at 2pm work?"

## Phone + Email Combo

- Phone is primary channel for Reisert's motion; email/LinkedIn support cadence
- LinkedIn view before first dial; email after voicemail
- Coordinate touches so each channel references the others
- Use Phone Intent scoring (TitanX / Phone Ready Leads lineage) before scaling dials

### Sample multi-touch cadence

| Day | Touch |
|---|---|
| 1 | LinkedIn view + connect |
| 2 | Cold call #1 (Wed or late afternoon) |
| 3 | Email referencing call |
| 5 | Cold call #2 |
| 7 | LinkedIn DM |
| 9 | Cold call #3 |
| 11 | Value-add email |
| 14 | Final call + breakup email |

By attempt 3: ~93% of conversations happen. By attempt 5: ~98%. Stop at 5; revisit in 90 days.

## Call Block Setup Checklist

- [ ] CRM contact stages map to Buckets 1–4
- [ ] Bucket 4 confirmations scheduled first in calendar block
- [ ] Bucket 3 list pre-loaded with 2–3× daily call plan
- [ ] Bucket 2 cadence tasks queued in SEP/CRM
- [ ] Bucket 1 contacts researched with dial notes before block starts
- [ ] Target 100+ meaningful activities/day (calls + emails + LI — not empty dials)
- [ ] Join CallBlitz or internal blitz for accountability (optional)

## When Phone Beats Email

| Phone-first | Email-first |
|---|---|
| Validated phone-intent list | Low connect-rate segments |
| Bucket 3 priority follow-ups | Bucket 2 early cadence touches |
| Objection handling in real time | Complex technical context |
| CallBlitz skill reps | Async enterprise buyers |

---

## Anti-Patterns

1. **Forward-only workflow** (Uncontacted → Scheduled in one jump) — skips Priority/Working insight
2. **Dials without bucket discipline** — reps chase new names while meetings no-show
3. **No CRM stage clarity** — reps cannot work backwards
4. **Parallel dialer without intent** — burns reputation and TAM
5. **Pitching on first call** — diagnose for next step, do not close
